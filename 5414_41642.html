Rice Ridge Fire Update - September 22,2017

Incident: Rice Ridge Fire Wildfire
Released: 7 min. ago

Fire origin: July 24, 2017, 1.4 miles northeast of Seeley Lake Montana.

Fire Information: (406) 813-0527 Hours: 8am – 8pm

Email: RiceRidgeFire@gmail.com

Websites: Inciweb: https://inciweb.nwcg.gov/incident/5414
Facebook: Lolo National Forest Facebook Page

Diane Hutton’s Northern Rockies Type 2 Wildland Fire Management Team took command of the fire at 6:00 p.m. yesterday.

Summary of yesterday’s actions: Assessments and identification of repair work from fire line suppression in the northwestern corner of the fire perimeter is complete. Rehab work continues in the Morell Lookout area. Smoke is still visible in the Findell and Murphy Creek lobes. Heat is still holding in Sawyer Creek and in the bog area between Blind Canyon Creek and Swamp Creek. Fire fighters continued to use sprinkler lines to lessen the heat, recharge the bog and decrease exposure to the snag hazard. Crews continued to mop-up, patrol, and remove slash along the fire’s perimeter.

Today’s planned activities: Light rain and cooler temps will limit fire behavior to creeping and smoldering in heavy fuels. Smoke still may be visible within the fire’s perimeter. Crews will continue to patrol and mop-up along the containment lines and demobilize excess equipment. Areas of repair will continue to be identified. Removal of slash will continue on private lands in the Doney Lake and Blackfoot Clearwater area. Work will start on the Nordic ski areas and on the roads toward the Morrell Lookout. Fire activity in the Bob Marshall Wilderness Complex is under monitoring status.

Completed·: The suppression strategy for this fire is full suppression on 23% of the fire perimeter and confinement on 12% of the fire perimeter for a total of 35%. The remaining 65% of the fire perimeter is located in remote backcountry and wilderness and will be monitored for any activity as fall weather arrives. Today’s “Completed” percentage in the summary table above indicates that 72% of the full suppression/confinement actions taken on the fire have been completed.

Closures: Multiple closures related to fire suppression efforts are in effect for public lands managed by the Lolo and Flathead National Forests, Montana DNRC, and Montana Fish, Wildlife and Parks. Visit the Lolo and Flathead National Forests webpages for more information for closures on National Forests lands. Visit fwp.mt.gov or call 406-542-5500 for up-to-date information on Fish, Wildlife and Park land and hunting closures.

Jurisdiction: The Lolo National Forest and Montana Department of Natural Resources and Conservation.

Cooperators: Missoula and Powell County Sheriffs, Flathead National Forest, Seeley Lake, Ovando and Greenough-Potomac Fire Departments, Montana FWP, Montana Highway Patrol, Montana Department of Transportation, Missoula and Powell County Commissioners, Bureau of Land Management, Blackfoot Challenge.
