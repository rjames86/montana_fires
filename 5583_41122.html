Highway 200 Complex Daily Update, September 15, 8:00 a.m.

Incident: Highway 200 Complex Wildfire
Released: 1 day ago

Related Information

  • Highway 200 Complex Update, September 15 (pdf 311 kb)

Approximate Size (acres): Sheep Gap – 20,497 Deep & Miller Creek – 14,126 Cub Creek – 4,992 Readers – 152

Total personnel: 241

Location: The Highway 200 Complex fires are located in Sanders County, MT near the towns of Thompson Falls and Plains. The Sheep Gap fire is approximately 2 miles west of Plains. The remaining complex fires are located 8 miles north and west of Thompson Falls.

Evacuations and Closures: The Sanders County Sheriff's Office conducted evacuations on Swamp Creek Road from Four Corners to the intersection of the East and West fork of Swamp Creek. Pre-evacuations were completed from Four Corners to 132 Blackjack Rd. and from 132 Blackjack Rd. to where it intersects River Rd West. Additional areas under evacuation orders include Glades Ranch, Cat Track Lane to Mountain subdivision and the residences between south of Blackjack Road, and Foothill Lane. These areas have been closed by the Sheriff’s office. Members of the Montana Army National Guard are assisting with road closures, pre-evacuations, evacuations, and any other assistance that may be needed. The Forest Service has also placed area closure orders around the fires as they are integral to safe fire operations. Visit the Inciweb Closures tab for details and map of the closures.

Fire Update: Fire behavior calmed today with the arrival of the predicted storm system. Although no rain was seen on the fire areas, cloud cover, cooler temperatures, and higher humidity significantly decreased fire activity. Firefighters were able to keep holding the fires where safe to do so and scout for opportunities to corral the latest fire spread. Crews also continue to identify and protect structures and values at risk. As other fires in Montana receive wetting rain and are brought under better control, released resources are being procured for use amongst the ongoing fires.

Weather and Fire Behavior: As the low pressure system moves through the area, temperatures are predicted to remain cooler today (upper 50s to lower 60s) with a corresponding rise in humidity. The weekend forecast calls for a slight warming trend but is not expected to be as unseasonably warm and dry as it has been. There is chance for frost overnight with slight potentials for rain. Fire behavior will diminish with the weather but the severity of dry vegetation conditions and prevalent topography are likely to continue fire spread.


Fire Details

Sheep Gap – Dozers, engines, and handcrews were used yesterday to corral the large fire expansion on the southeast edge of this fire. A line was scouted and a plan made to continue these efforts today. Firefighters and heavy equipment will continue to provide structure protection along the east flank of the fire. They will also patrol for, and suppress, any spot fires that resulted from the increased fire activity. Pre-evacuation and evacuation orders are in place as described above.

Deep Creek/Miller Creek – Fire spread is expected to slow today. Firefighters are working to hold the fire from crossing Deep Creek and preventing growth to the north. Sprinkler systems were being constructed to protect wooden bridges in the area. A pre-evacuation order is still in effect east of the Clark Fork River in the Blue Slide Area.

Cub Creek – Limited surface fire will support firefighters in the implementation of ongoing tactics. Fire will move downslope into Emma and Clear Creek and Little Beaver drainage. Firefighters will continue to control and hold the fire at roads 2262, 301 and Clear Creek. Pre-Evacuation orders are in effect for an area west of Highway 200 from White Pine Creek to Cherry Creek.

Reader Fires – The Reader 1 and Reader 2 fires continue to hold at existing fire lines. Fire activity will be minimal creeping and smoldering. Engines will continue to patrol and mop-up as needed to keep lines secure.
