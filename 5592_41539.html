Visit Lincoln County Webpage www.lincolncountymt.us to sign-up for the County Code Red system.

Public and firefighter safety continues to be our #1 priority. There will not be any further night patrols on either fire.

Weather: Cloudy skies with scattered showers will dominate the general area this week. Today, temperatures are only expected to reach the high 40s with light south winds of 5-10 mph with minimum RH’s around 65%. Rain accumulations on both fire areas have ranged between 1.00 to 1.25 inches over the past 48 hours.

West Fork Fire Yesterday: Fire managers increased containment to 55% that include the areas of lower Quartz Creek, Bobtail Creek and upper Pipe Creek road near 17 mile junction. Firefighters patrolled and mopped up on most edges of the fire perimeter. Engines had limited accessibility to fire area due to slick, muddy roads. Back haul of unneeded equipment from the firelines has begun. An approximate 3 mile section of dozer line and mechanized fuel break has been identified to add to the containment strategy. This section is from the 4696 road near the Bobtail Cutoff road, north to the 4613 road near Noisy Creek. Crews were working to remove danger trees weakened by recent fire and weather conditions along public access routes.

The residents at 17 mile/ Pipe Creek road have had their evacuation status downgraded to pre-evacuation and were allowed to return home. The access to the 17 mile area up Pipe Creek road was opened to residents only with pilot cars.

Today’s Planned Actions: With the continued wet weather, firefighters will concentrate on patrolling, holding, and improving all containment lines. Muddy, slick roads may affect firefighter access to all fire areas again. The spot fire near Gold Hill will continue to be monitored. Rehabilitation needs for dozer lines are being identified and completed.

West Fork Fire Evacuations: Some evacuation orders have been lifted. Please check the Lincoln County Sheriff’s Facebook page or call (406) 293-6295 for the most up-to-date information on evacuations.

West Fork Fire Closures: The Kootenai National Forest has issued a closure order in effect for the West Fork Fire. The closure order and map are located under the closure tab on the Inciweb page.

Moose Peak Yesterday: Fire managers increased containment to 67% that include the areas around Miller Lake, Bayhorse Creek, and McGinnis Meadows. Firefighters concentrated on patrolling, holding, and improving all containment lines. Muddy, slick roads affected firefighter access to all fire areas. Back haul of unneeded equipment has begun.

Today’s Planned Actions: With the continued wet weather, firefighters will concentrate on patrolling, holding, and improving all hand and dozer lines established in the East Fisher Creek, McGinnis Creek and Fishtrap Creek areas. Muddy, slick roads may affect firefighter access to all fire areas again.

Moose Peak Fire Pre - Evacuations: Pre-Evacuation notices have been issued for East Fisher Road, Bayhorse Ranch and McGinnis Meadows. Please check the Lincoln County Sheriff’s Facebook page or call (406) 293-6295 for the most up-to-date information on evacuations.

Moose Peak Fire Closures: The Kootenai National Forest has issued a closure order in effect for the Moose Peak Fire area. Sylvan Lake campground has been closed. The closure order and map are located under the closure tab on the Inciweb page.
