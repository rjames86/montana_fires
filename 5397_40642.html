Meyers Sapphire Update for September 10, 2017

Incident: Meyers Fire Wildfire
Released: 23 min. ago

Related Information

  • Meyers Sapphire Update for 09.10.17 (pdf 135 kb)

A public meeting will be held on Tuesday, September 12 at 6:30 p.m. at the Wise River Community Center east of the Meyers Fire.

Despite red flag conditions yesterday, fire activity was low for most parts of the Meyers Fire and the Sapphire Complex.

Sapphire Complex: 43,642 acres Location: Approximately 25 miles south-southeast of Missoula Containment: 59% Cause: Lightning Total Personnel: 238

Three fires comprise the Sapphire Complex: Little Hogback, Goat Creek, and Sliderock. The fires are in steep, rocky terrain between Rock Creek and Upper Willow Creek drainages.

Little Hogback Fire: 34,442 acres

Some mop-up continues on hot spots along the southwest and south sides. Isolated areas of heat remain along the remainder of the perimeter; crews will continue monitoring for and addressing hot spots as needed. Unburned pockets in the interior continue to burn, especially in the northwest part of the fire well away from the perimeter. Structure protection along Rock Creek Road remains a priority. Incident managers hope to shift from mop-up and patrolling to monitoring soon.

Goat Creek Fire: 8,300 acres

No substantial growth has been detected. Significant heat remains only in the interior and at a few spots on the east flank. Firefighters have shifted from mop-up to rehabilitation of constructed fire breaks.

Sliderock Fire: 900 acres

There has been no growth in the overall size of this fire; any hot spots are in the fire interior. Rehabilitation of constructed firelines will follow after this work is completed for the Goat Creek Fire.

Meyers Fire: 60,254 acres Location: Approximately 25 miles southwest of Philipsburg, Montana Containment: 0% Cause: Lightning Total Personnel: 395

The fire is burning in mixed conifer stands, primarily lodgepole pine, Douglas-fir, and subalpine fir, with many standing dead trees. These snags have become increasingly dangerous to firefighters as they burn through and fall with stronger winds. There are residences still at risk including those in the Moose Lake, Frog Pond, Billie Springer Memorial Park and Bonanza Lands areas.

The fire spotted north across Carpp Creek in the eastern part of the fire, but with diminished smoke cover, helicopters were able to make water drops there to keep the spot fires in check. Crews continued to mop-up and secure some areas of concern around the fire, including southeast of Zeke’s Meadows, and on the west flank including the Frog Pond area, which had minimal activity yesterday. Fireline construction is about 2/3 complete around the East Fork Bitterroot communities. Exposed rocky areas have slowed growth at the south end of the fire.

There are two areas of the fire located in the Wise River Ranger District. One area, between Howell Creek and Beaver Creek in Beaverhead County, was active yesterday but showed minimal growth. The second area, between West Fork Fishtrap Creek and West Fork LaMarche Creek in Deerlodge County, is burning in a very rocky area with sparse fuels. These areas will continue to be monitored. Necessary preparations are being taken to protect structures that could be affected.

Structure protection continues to be the priority on the fire after firefighter and public safety. For Sunday, firefighters will focus on the northeast part of the fire and the Carpp Creek drainage, concentrating on keeping the spot fires detected yesterday in check. Firefighters will continue to improve firelines in the Billie Springer Memorial and Bonanza subdivisions as well as other private properties in the area, with hopes of completing these lines in the next two or three days. The southeast part of the fire will be patrolled primarily by air, weather permitting. Firefighters may burn out small pockets of unburned fuel southeast of Zeke’s Meadows to better secure that border, given safe conditions. Across the divide to the southeast, structure protection is being completed on the Bender and Mystic Lake cabins.

Resources Threatened

Sapphire Complex: Private property and structures in the Rock Creek, Upper Willow Creek, and Marshall Creek drainages remain threatened. Other resources threatened include fisheries in Rock Creek, grazing lands along the southern boundary of the Little Hogback Fire, Bonneville Power Administration powerline, and a communication site.

Meyers Fire: Private property and structures in the Moose Lake, Frog Pond, Billie Springer Memorial Park, Bonanza Lake areas, as well as other scattered individual dwellings continue to be monitored and prepped. Nearby grazing lands also remain a concern.

Evacuations

Sapphire Complex: A mandatory evacuation (Level Red) order for approximately thirty-five residences along Rock Creek Road remains in effect, beginning from and including Wild Rose Loop south to Stony Creek. Other pre-evacuation (Level Yellow) notices are still in effect. For information on evacuation planning refer to the Ready-Set-Go materials available at http://www.wildlandfirersg.org/Portals/18/Resources/States/MT/RSG_Montana_ActionGuide_web.pdf

Meyers Fire: The Frog Pond and Moose Lake areas are still under an evacuation order. Additionally, an evacuation warning is in effect on East Fork Rd from FS Rd 5778 to Little East Fork Rd (FS RD 724) on the Bitterroot National Forest. Those under an evacuation warning should be prepared to leave at a moment’s notice.

Closures

Sapphire Complex: The Rock Creek Road from I-90 south to Bitterroot Campground (MP 23.5) is OPEN. Due to fire traffic congestion and fire safety concerns, Rock Creek Road will stay CLOSED from Bitterroot Campground, south to Cornish Gulch (FSR5027), and public traffic is NOT allowed. Upper Willow Creek Road is currently closed to the public. The Beaverhead-Deerlodge National Forest reduced closure restrictions in the Black Pine ridge area, but maintains all area closures in Upper Willow Creek, North Fork Lower Willow Creek, and Harvey Creek areas. The Bureau of Land Management has an area closure in Upper Willow Creek, Ram Mountain, and nearby areas. The Lolo and Beaverhead- Deerlodge National Forests, the Bureau of Land Management, and the Montana Department of Natural Resources (DNRC) closure orders and maps are posted under the closures tab on Inciweb. A temporary flight restriction (TFR) remains in effect surrounding the Little Hogback Fire area.

Meyers Fire: Bitterroot National Forest emergency closure orders are still in effect for upper areas of the East Fork Bitterroot River. The expanded Beaverhead-Deerlodge National Forest closure also remains in effect. On the south side of the fire, the closure extends between Bender Creek and LaMarche Creek, including Pintler Campground and Trailhead, Mystic Trailhead, Mussigbrod Campground and Trailhead, and all trailheads in the Mudd Creek and Fishtrap Creek areas. Several miles of the Continental Divide Trail are closed. A temporary flight restriction (TFR) is still in place surrounding the Meyers Fire area. Granite County is under Stage 2 fire restrictions for private lands in the county. Amended Stage 1 fire restrictions are in effect prohibiting ALL campfires in southwestern Montana on public lands, including in fire rings and developed recreation areas. For fire restriction details, visit firerestrictions.us/mt.

Please adhere to all closures and allow our firefighters to perform their jobs safely.

Weather

Building high pressure will bring warmer temperatures, lower humidity, and diminished winds. Today, precipitation chance is 10%, with high temps in the upper 70s to low 80s, and humidity bottoming out about 22%. Southwest winds will be 10-15 mph in the morning, increasing to 15-20 mph. Southwesterly ridgetop winds are predicted at 15-20 mph. A stronger overnight inversion will persist later in the day tomorrow, and will bring near or below-freezing overnight lows below the inversion. Consequently, low areas should hold more smoke.

For further information, visit https://inciweb.nwcg.gov and select “Meyers Fire” or “Sapphire Complex” in Montana, and follow the Beaverhead-Deerlodge National Forest Facebook, Bitterroot National Forest Facebook or Lolo National Forest pages.
