Second Day of Large Burnout Operation Begins

Incident: Rice Ridge Fire Wildfire
Released: 27 min. ago

SECOND DAY OF LARGE AERIAL IGNITION BURNOUT
BEGINS ON SOUTH SIDE OF RICE RIDGE FIRE


For the second day in a row, weather conditions have been favorable to continue a burnout operation this afternoon on the south side of the Rice Ridge fire on the ridges above Forest Road 477 west of Monture Cabin. The purpose of the operation is to bring the uncontrolled fire edge down to the fire line. Forest Road 477 has been prepped for use as the primary control line. Aerial ignition will be completed by a helicopter using an aerial ignition dispenser (ping pong ball method).

In addition to the road surface, a 100-foot wide shaded fuel break has been completed upslope of the road towards the main fire area. Excess vegetation has been removed from this area and hauled off-site. The shaded fuel break will serve as an additional buffer between the main fire and the road.

This is the second day of what is planned to be a three day project to get the uncontrolled fire edge from the top of the mountain to the road where firefighters will have a safer location to control the fire.

Smoke from the operation will be very visible from the Highway 200 corridor east of Clearwater Junction, northeast of Kozy Korner and northwest of Ovando. Depending on location, the fireline varies in distance from 5 to 7 miles north of Highway 200.

Ridges running north to south from the main east – west ridge at the top of the mountain had fire started on them yesterday. This fire has been backing downslope east and west off the ridge tops and into the canyon bottoms, slowly moving towards the fire line as a low intensity fire. Smoke may appear to be coming from multiple locations between Shanley Creek/Black Canyon on the west and Cave Creek/Dunham Creek on the east, a span of 6 miles in width. The total acreage of the burnout will be approximately 5,000 -6,000 acres. The majority of the burnout will take place on National Forest System lands. A very small area of the Blackfoot-Clearwater Wildlife Management Area (approximately 40 acres) will also be impacted as fire approaches Forest Road 477 between Black Canyon and Dry Cottonwood Creek on the southwest corner of the burnout.

Aircraft will be available for water drops to keep fire intensity in check on the mountainside. Firefighters and engines will be on the fire line as the fire moves towards the road. Night crews will be patrolling the fire line throughout the duration of the burnout operation both today, tomorrow and until final mop up along Forest Road 477 is completed.

The public will be notified of any change in the burnout plan.
