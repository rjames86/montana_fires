·Community Meeting at 6:00 p.m. at Libby Christian Church, 100 Kootenai River Road·

Weather: Yesterday’s cloud cover helped moderate the expected high winds. Today is suspected to be similar to yesterday with clearer skies and lower wind gust. Temperatures will be in the mid 70’s with minimum relative humidity around 30 percent. Winds will be out of the southwest, 5-15 mile per hour.

West Fork Fire Yesterday: Expected winds did not materialize over the fire. A 3.2 acre spot fire was contained ½ of a mile east of the fireline. This spot was located in the Bobtail Road area, and crews worked the fire through the night. Along Quartz Creek Road, the fire backed down to the 600 road. Engines patrolling the area were able to locate and contain spotfires that had started on the west side of Quartz Creek Road. There was active fire behavior near Mount Tom. The fire did not move beyond the 471 Road. Crews improved the dozer line from the north end of Bobtail Road east to Pipe Creek Road, using old logging roads.

Today’s Planned Actions: Today will be the last day for the Type 1 Hot Shot Crew. They will be utilized to complete the burnout operation in the Bobtail Ridge area. A Strike Team of engines from Flathead County will assist with structure protection along Pipe Creek Road and Bobtail Road. Engines will monitor and hold the fire perimeter on Quartz Creek Road. Heavy logging equipment will move to the northeast side of the fire, along Pipe Creek road, to begin constructing fireline. If conditions and equipment allow, dozer line will be constructed closer to the fireline.

Fire Behavior: Fire may remain active as a wind event is projected to continue especially if the fire progresses to the northeast where wind influence will be more pronounced.

West Fork Fire Evacuations: Evacuation orders remain the same Please check the Lincoln County Sheriff’s Facebook page or call (406) 293-6295 for the most up-to-date information on evacuations.

West Fork Fire Closures: The Kootenai National Forest has issued a closure order in effect for the West Fork Fire. The map and order is located under the closure tab on the InciWeb Page.

Moose Peak Yesterday: Clear skies allowed helicopters to assist firefighters on the ground. The area east of Fisher Creek near Miller Lake, north end of Fishtrap Creek and south of McGinnis Meadows expected active fire behavior. The checkline slowed the fires progression allowing firefighters time to construct a primary dozer line to McGinnis Meadows.

Today’s Planned Actions: Firefighters will continue constructing fireline using old logging roads away from the active fire. These firelines are being constructed east of Sylvan Lake to Fisher Creek and west of Sylvan Lake to south of McGinnis Meadows.

Fire Behavior: Fire will remain active as the wind event is projected to continue, especially where winds are aligned with Vermillion Creek to the northeast.

Moose Peak Fire Pre - Evacuations: Evacuation notices have been issued for East Fisher Rd and Bayhorse Ranch. Pre-Evacuation notices have been issued for McGinnis Meadows. Please check the Lincoln County Sheriff’s Facebook page or call (406) 293-6295 for the most up-to-date information on evacuations.

Moose Peak Fire Closures: Sylvan Lake campground has been closed as well as. The Kootenai National Forest has issued a closure order in effect for the Moose Peak Fire. The map and order is located under the closure tab on the InciWeb Page.
