Community Meeting at 6:00 p.m. at Libby Christian Church 100 Kootenai River Road

Visit Lincoln County Webpage www.lincolncountymt.us to sign-up for the County Code Red system.

Weather: The inversion this morning is expected to be dense, and then break at approximately 1:30pm. Winds will remain light; therefore, dense smoke will continue to settle in the area. The high pressure will move off to our southeast today. Temperatures and relative humidity will be similar to yesterday. Monsoonal moisture will stream into the region tomorrow (Friday) bringing a chance of showers and thunderstorms to the area. These storms will be isolated in nature and mostly dry as they cross northwestern Montana.

West Fork Fire Planned Actions: The planned burnout yesterday was successfully completed to Lindy Peak. This operation was done in the Bobtail Ridge and Bobtail Ridge Road area. Crews today will monitor/hold what was burned east of the Quartz /US F.S. 600 Road. A crew patrolled/monitored the burnout area throughout the night. When conditions allow, the burnout will continue from Lindy Peak south to the junction of Quartz Road and the Bobtail Ridge Road. The purpose of the burnout is to increase the buffer between the active fire and the residence in the area to obtain this objective, crews will light a low intensity, backing fire along the dozer line behind the homes on Bobtail Ridge Road. As resources arrive the Pipe Creek road will be improved as an indirect fireline. A dozer line has been identified to tie in Pipe Creek Road to the fire. The structure defense group will be preparing homes in the event of the fire becoming more active. Actions will include moving flammable materials away from the homes and installing sprinklers when supplies and crews arrive.

West Fork Fire Evacuations: New evacuation orders have issued, please check the Lincoln County Sheriff’s Facebook page or call (406) 293-6295 for the most up-to-date information on evacuations.

West Fork Fire Closures: The Kootenai Forest has issued a closure order in effect for the West Fork Fire. The map and order is located under the closure tap on the InciWeb Page.

Moose Peak Fire Planned Actions: Dozers will be constructing fireline today from Fishtrap Creek to Sylvan Lake; when appropriate and safe. As more equipment arrives, they will be placed in previous scouted areas to achieve fire manager’s objectives to protect values at risk. The Structure Defense crews will continue structure assessments on the Fisher Creek and McGinnis Meadows homes. As supplies and crews arrive, plans are in place to begin installing sprinklers around homes. Three spot fires grew together, and now form a part of the main fire.

Moose Peak Fire Pre - Evacuations: Please check the Lincoln County Sheriff’s Facebook page or call (406) 293-6295 for the most up-to-date information on evacuations.

Moose Peak Fire Closures: The Kootenai Forest has issued a closure order in effect for the Moose Peak Fire. The map and order is located under the closure tab on the InciWeb Page.
