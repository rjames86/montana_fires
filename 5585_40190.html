Visit Lincoln County Webpage www.lincolncountymt.us to sign-up for the County Code Red system.

Weather: The inversion this morning is expected to be dense and lift later in the day. Winds will remain light; therefore, dense smoke will continue to settle in the area. Temperatures will be higher than yesterday and relative humidity will be lower.

West Fork Fire Planned Actions: The planned burnout for last night was postponed, due to the increase in humidity. The burnout operation will resume when conditions allow. Burnout operations are only done under certain weather and fuel conditions to ensure the fire behavior is minimal. This operation is planned for the Bobtail ridge and Bobtail Ridge Road area. Crews will light down the Bobtail Ridge and the Bobtail Ridge Road simultaneously from the north to the south. Firefighters will not be lighting off the dozer lines near the homes on Bobtail Road. The purpose of the burnout is to increase the buffer between the active fire and the residence in the area, allowing for quicker re-entry into the evacuation area. The structure defense group will be preparing homes in the event of the fire becoming more active. These crews will be moving flammable materials away from the homes and installing sprinklers when supplies arrive.

West Fork Fire Evacuations: No additional evacuations have been issued; please check the Lincoln County Sheriff’s Facebook page or call (406) 293-6295 for the most up-to-date information on evacuations.

West Fork Fire Closures: The Kootenai Forest has issued a closure order in effect for the West Fork Fire. The map and order is located under the closure tap on the InciWeb Page.

Moose Peak Fire Planned Actions: Fire managers are working to obtain resources necessary to complete fire objectives on the Moose Peak Fire. Potential fire lines are being scouted on all areas of the fire, as appropriate and available equipment will be utilized to construct indirect and direct firelines. The Structure Defense crews will continue structure assessments on the Fisher Creek and McGinnis Meadows homes. As supplies arrive, crews will begin installing sprinklers around homes in the event of the fire behavior increasing.

Moose Peak Fire Pre - Evacuations: No additional pre-evacuations and evacuations have been issued; please check the Lincoln County Sheriff’s Facebook page or call (406) 293-6295 for the most up-to-date information on evacuations.

Moose Peak Fire Closures: The Kootenai Forest has issued a closure order in effect for the Moose Peak Fire. The map and order is located under the closure tab on the InciWeb Page.
