Green Ridge Complex Trail Closure

Incident: Green Ridge Complex Wildfire
Released: 32 min. ago

BITTERROOT NATIONAL FOREST

1801 N. First Street

Hamilton, MT 59840

Forest Supervisor's Order

EMERGENCY FIRE CLOSURE – Green Ridge Complex Fire

Pursuant to Title 36 Code of Federal Regulations 261.50(b), the following act is prohibited as described below within the West Fork Ranger District of the Bitterroot National Forest. These restrictions are in addition to those enumerated in Subpart A, Part 261, Title 36, Code of Federal Regulations, and become effective upon signing and will remain in effect until rescinded.

The purpose of this order is to protect the public from potential harm from wildfires and firefighting operations associated with the Green Ridge Complex Fire.

36 CFR 261.53 – Special Closures:

It is prohibited to enter or be upon any area which is closed for the protection of “Public Health and Safety”:

(Title 36 CFR 261.53(e)).

36 CFR 261.55 – National Forest System Trails:

All persons are prohibited from being on any National Forest System Trail as described below:

(Title 36 CFR 261.55(a))

The trail closure description follows with a map attachment Description of Trails:

Within the Selway-Bitterroot Wilderness

NFST #40, West of the intersection of NFST # 40 & 4

NFST #3, West of Pack Bridge south of Indian Creek Campground

NFST #536, East of the boundary of the Bitterroot National Forest and Nez Perce – Clearwater National Forest

Exemptions:

Pursuant to 36 CFR 261.50(e), the following are exempt from this order:

1. Persons with a permit issued by a forest officer specifically authorizing the otherwise prohibited act or omission.

2. Any Federal, State, or local officer, or member of an organized rescue or firefighting force in the performance of an official duty.

3. Persons engaged in business, trade, or occupation in the area.

Signed in Hamilton, Montana, this 5th day of September, 2017.

Done at Hamilton, Montana this 5th day of September, 2017 and effective immediately until rescinded.

By: _________________________

JULIE K. KING

Forest Supervisor

Bitterroot National Forest

Penalty

Violation of these prohibitions is punishable by a fine of not more than $5,000 for an individual or $10,000 for an organization, or imprisonment for not more than six (6) months, or both (16 USC 551 and 18 USC 3559 and 3571).

Notification

A copy of this order shall be posted as prescribed within 36 CFR 261.51.
