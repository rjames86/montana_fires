Daily update for Crucifixion Creek, Strawberry Creek, and Scalp Fires, 9/15

Incident: Crucifixion Creek Fire Wildfire
Released: 1 day ago

Wildfire Information

Crucifixion Creek, Strawberry Creek, & Scalp Fires

Rocky Mountain Ranger District

Friday, September 15, 2017

Contact: Rocky Mountain Ranger District, 406-466-5341

Media Contact: Duane Buchi, (406) 750-8934

Location: CRUCIFIXION CREEK Approximately 10 miles west of Heart Butte, in the Badger-Two Medicine area; STRAWBERRY CREEK—Eastern edge of fire is approximately ½ mile east of Swift Dam and extends south to Sheep Creek on the state land, 17 miles west of Dupuyer; SCALP—Approximately 7 miles west of Teton Pass Ski Area.

Fire Management: The moisture has greatly reduced fire activity for all three fires. Updated GIS acreages are provided for each fire below. Our remote weather stations indicate moisture levels on the fires to date are from between one tenth to one quarter inch of precipitation. A slow, consistent event is the most effective in cooling fire areas. All area closures remain in place. There are no evacuations and all remaining road closures were lifted this morning. The fire is managed by a Type 3 Incident Command Team. Crews are working on equipment refurbishment in preparation should fire activity pick up again. Crews and equipment operators will limit travel to and from the fire area to avoid unnecessary exposure to hazards created with the moisture and lessen the impact of heavy truck traffic on muddy roads.

Crucifixion Creek Fire Update (11,003 Acres): No new activity to report.

Strawberry Creek Fire Update (30,461 Acres): The Rocky Mountain Ranger district hosted a public meeting in the Dupuyer Community Hall yesterday evening to highlight fire history, current status, and future expectations. 40 community members were in attendance, including a number of community cooperators.

Scalp Fire Update (20,406 Acres): No new activity to report.

Heart Butte Air Quality: Air quality across the Rocky Mountain Front, to include Heart Butte is forecasted as “Good” today. A smoke monitor is in place at Heart Butte, and information can be accessed through this site: https://wrcc.dri.edu/cgi-bin/smoke.pl. Click on the red location bubble that is numbered #3015 for information on the Heart Butte area. This data is time delayed by 6 hours.

Weather: Widespread wetting rains and higher-elevation accumulating snow will continue through today and into the overnight before ending from west to east Saturday. The development of a lee-side trough will help to produce some southerly to southwest winds across the mountains and plains late Saturday night into Sunday, and also help to produce a significant warming and drying trend as weak upper ridging moves overhead. However, this significant warming and drying trend will not come close to producing critical fire weather conditions. A cooler and wetter pattern looks to continue through at least the next week as a series of disturbances looks poised to move across our region.

Public Meeting: Wednesday, September 20, 2017 at 6 p.m. in the Heart Butte School-Multi-Purpose Room

Additional Information: A PUBLIC AUTOMATED INFORMATION LINE was established for Alice Creek, Park Creek, Crucifixion Creek, Strawberry Creek, Scalp, & Rice Ridge Fires. This number is (406) 495-3767.
