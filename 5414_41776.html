Rice Ridge Fire Update - September 25th, 2017

Incident: Rice Ridge Fire Wildfire
Released: 20 min. ago

Related Information

  • Printable Update for September 25th (pdf 271 kb)

Community meetings are scheduled as followed:
Seeley Lake – Tonight, Monday, September 25th at 6pm, Mission Bible Fellowship Church
Ovando - Tuesday, September 26th at 6pm, Ovando Elementary School

Closures: Road, trail and area closures are still in effect. Local Forest managers, in coordination with fire operations personnel, are evaluating the potential to modify area closures daily. Public should respect closures due to heavy equipment working in the area and the hazards associated with weakened trees and snags. Visit the Lolo and Flathead National Forests webpages for more information on closures on National Forests lands. Visit fwp.mt.gov or call 406-542-5500 for up-to-date information on Fish, Wildlife and Park land and hunting closures.

Summary of yesterday’s actions: Half of the Lewis & Clark Hotshots and the Seeley Ranger District trail crew worked to clear the North Fork Blackfoot trail. The other half of the Hotshots gridded the area near the Monture Guard Station, locating and extinguishing residual heat. During the aerial reconnaissance flight, fire managers observed smoke in the White Creek, Babcock Creek and Youngs Creek drainages in the Wilderness. Other areas that continue to smolder include Findell Creek, McCabe Creek, and the area east of the Monture Guard Station; no smokes were visible in the “swamp” area. These areas of activity are interior to the control lines. Crews and equipment have progressed with repair of machine fire lines along the southern boundary of the fire. Over the last four days, 34.5 miles of fire line have been repaired, inspected, and marked as complete.

Today’s planned activities: In addition to continuing the mop-up, patrol, slash hauling, and repair work, crews will be falling snags, grinding piles and blading the roads in preparation for opening the forest to public use. Firefighters will continue working on the North Fork Blackfoot trail and the areas of residual heat near the Monture Guard Station. Crews and equipment will be moving to the Double Arrow area to begin suppression repair work there.

Weather forecast: Today will bring a mix of clouds and sun, with temperatures in the upper 50s. Tuesday will see the tail end of a cold front, bringing additional clouds to the area. Temperatures are expected to be warmer later this week and may lead to increased fire behavior in isolated islands of unburned fuels interior to the control lines. Winds to 15 mph and relative humidity levels near 20-25% may materialize by Friday.

Completed·: The suppression strategy for this fire is full suppression on 23% of the fire perimeter and confinement on 12% of the fire perimeter for a total of 35%. The remaining 65% of the fire perimeter is located in remote backcountry and wilderness and will be monitored for any activity as fall weather arrives. Today’s “Completed” percentage in the summary table above indicates that 72% of the full suppression/confinement actions taken on the fire have been completed.
