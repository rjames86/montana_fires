Moose Peak and West Fork Fire Daily Update 9/5/2017

Incident: Moose Peak Wildfire
Released: 24 min. ago

Fire Information Number: (406) 209-9961

Location: West Fork/Mount Tom is 7 miles northwest of Libby, MT Size: 5,760

Cause: Lightning Containment: 0% Personnel: 54

Email: WestForkMoosePeakFires@gmail.com InciWeb: https:// inciweb.nwcg.gov/incident/5585/

Location: Moose Peak Fire originated near Moose Peak in Sanders County, MT Size: 4,993

Cause: Lightning Containment: 0% Personnel: 3

Email: WestForkMoosePeakFires@gmail.com InciWeb: https://inciweb.nwcg.gov/incident/5583/

The Rocky Mountain Area Incident Management Blue Team, Jay Esperance Incident Commander, assumed command of the West Fork/Mount Tom and Moose Peak Fires this morning at 6:00am.

Visit Lincoln County Webpage www.lincolncountymt.us to sign-up for the County Code Red system.

Weather: Skies are expected to be clear today, however smoke in the area will shade the fire area moderating fire behavior. Easterly winds are likely at the ridgetops with upslope/up-valley winds in the valleys and on the slopes. The inversion this morning will lift out of the valleys around noon.

West Fork/Mount Tom Fire Planned Actions: The West Fork and Mount Tom fires grew together last night. The fires in future will be referred to as the West Fork Fire. Firefighters will be working on USFS 600 constructing fire line and improving the road as a containment line. Dozers and other heavy equipment will be working north of the Bobtail Community connecting dozer lines to construct indirect containment lines, which will later be used to conduct burnout operations. The structure defense group will be providing point protection and evaluating values a risk.

West Fork Fire Evacuations: No additional evacuations have been issued; please check the Lincoln County Sheriff’s Facebook page or call (406) 293-6295 for the most up-to-date information on evacuations.

West Fork Fire Closures: The Kootenai Forest has issued a closure order in effect for the West Fork Fire. The map and order is located under the closure tap on the InciWeb Page.

Moose Peak Fire Planned Actions: The Moose Peak Fire originated in Sanders County on the Cabinet Ranger District on August 30. The Moose Peak Fire has previously been under the jurisdiction of the Highway 200 Complex, as of today it will be managed by the Rocky Mountain Area Blue Team. Today, firefighters will be scouting each division of the fire looking for areas where direct line construction will be appropriate. The structure defense group will be providing point protection and evaluating values a risk.

Moose Peak Fire Pre - Evacuations: No additional pre-evacuations and evacuations have been issued; please check the Lincoln County Sheriff’s Facebook page or call (406) 293-6295 for the most up-to-date information on evacuations.

Moose Peak Fire Closures: The Kootenai Forest has issued a closure order in effect for the Moose Peak Fire. The map and order is located under the closure tap on the InciWeb Page.
