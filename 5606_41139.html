Blacktail Fire Update 9-15-2017

Incident: Blacktail Fire Wildfire
Released: 1 day ago

Fire Information Line: (406) 333-1544

Follow Fire Information: Inciweb: https://inciweb.nwcg.gov/incident/5606/

Email: blacktailfire2017@gmail.com

Important – Drones are prohibited in the fires vicinity & have potential to shut down all operations.

Start date: September 10, 2017

Location: Southwest of Two Dot, Montana

Cause: Lightning

Size: 5,351

Containment: 15%

Total Personnel: 100 personnel

Resources Assigned: Cain’s Nevada Interagency Type III Incident Management Team assumed command of the Blacktail fire on September 12, 2017. This fire includes both Federal and Private Lands in Meagher, Wheatland and Sweet Grass Counties. Numerous engines are assigned, as well as, one Type 2 Initial Attack 20 - person hand crew as well as numerous pieces of heavy equipment. Firefighters efforts are supported by 2 Type 3 (Light) helicopters and 1 Type 2 (Medium) helicopter.

Fire History: The fire was reported at approximately 1500 hours on Sunday September 10, 2017. Nine smokejumpers initial attacked the fire and were assisted by local resources including volunteer fire departments from Two Dot, Melville, Wheatland and Sweet Grass counties. Firefighting efforts were assisted with two helicopter and single engine air tankers dropping retardant as needed. Suppression efforts have been hampered by high winds coupled with steep rocky terrain. Active fire behavior has been reported on the fire into the early morning hours.

Yesterday’s Operations: Crews were able to take advantage of the rainy conditions to put fireline in along the north fire perimeter. Additional crews were able to continue improving existing contingency lines along the east and south fire perimeters utilizing both heavy equipment as well as engines.

Planned Operations: Firefighters are continuing to improve identified contingency lines and hold and improve existing containment lines along the northern perimeter of the fire. Along the south firefighters will be improving existing contingency lines as well as construction of fireline to minimize fire spread to the south toward O’Hearn Creek.

Closures: A new closure has been put into place for the fire area. The boundary will run on the north side along the proclaimed Forest Boundary on the north side of the Crazy Mountains where Trail 636 intersects the Forest Boundary and then east and south following the Forest Boundary to the boundary of the Belt Creek-White Sulphur Springs Ranger District and the Yellowstone Ranger District. The closure area then traverses west-northwesterly on the boundary between the ranger districts to the junction of Trail 636, and then northward on and including Trail 636 to the point of origination on the north boundary. For more details on the closure and/or a closure map please check inciweb: https://inciweb.nwcg.gov/incident/5606/#

Weather: Showers will continue in the fire area today and tomorrow. Up to an inch of precipitation is expected. This is expected to significantly hamper fire activity, although it is not expected to extinguish the fire completely.
