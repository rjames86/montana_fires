Meyers Fire Update for September 7, 2017

Incident: Meyers Fire Wildfire
Released: 31 min. ago

Related Information

  • Meyers Fire Update September 7, 2017 (pdf 94 kb)

Current Size: 57,933 acres
Resources Assigned: 376

Status: The fire is burning in mixed conifer stands, primarily lodgepole pine, Douglas-fir, and subalpine fir, with numerous standing dead trees. There are residences still at risk in the Moose Lake, Frog Pond, Billy Springer Memorial Park and Bonanza Lands areas.

Yesterday’s Activities: Thick smoke laid across the fire limiting fire growth much of the day. Winds were favorable for burnout operations in Moose Lake and Zeke’s Meadows later in the day. Both burnout operations were successful in reducing fuels in these areas to limit future fire spread. Reconnaissance flights over the fire confirmed fire is active on the east side of the Continental Divide in Howell Creek. Firefighters started assessing structures and other values at risk in advance of the fire on the east side of the Divide. Crews were shared with the Sapphire Complex to assist with their burning operations. Mike Dueitt’s Southern Area Type 1 Team met with the Meyers Fire and Sapphire Complex teams, local land management agencies, and cooperators to begin transition of incident management.

Current Resources: Five 20-person crews, twenty-one engines, four pieces of heavy equipment (skidgines, dozers, masticators), ten water tenders, and three helicopters. Total personnel: 376.

Today’s Activities: The high pressure weather system that moved into the area will hold the smoke over the fire area. This will again limit fire activity. Most of the fire growth will occur due to slope and terrain, not wind. Structure protection is still the priority on the fire. Crews will patrol and mop up the previous day’s burnout operations to ensure no heat remains. Machine and hand line is still under construction on the East Fork of the Bitterroot around the 2 subdivisions. Firefighters will hold and improve lines in Frog Pond and conduct burnout operations where feasible. The Type 1 team will be shadowing both the Sapphire Complex and Meyers Fire personnel before taking command of the fires at 6:00 p.m.

Evacuations: The Frog Pond and Moose Lake areas are under an evacuation order. Additionally, an evacuation warning is in effect on East Fork Rd from FS Rd 5778 to Little East Fork Rd (FS RD 724) on the Bitterroot National Forest. Those under an evacuation warning should be prepared to leave at a moment’s notice.

Restrictions/Closures: Closure orders are in place on both National Forests. Bitterroot National Forest emergency closure orders are in effect for upper areas of the East Fork Bitterroot River. The Beaverhead-Deerlodge National Forest has expanded the emergency closure order around the fire. A temporary flight restriction (TFR) is in effect surrounding the Meyers Fire area. Granite County is under Stage 2 fire restrictions for private lands in the county. Amended Stage 1 fire restrictions are in effect today which prohibits ALL campfires in southwestern Montana on public lands, including in fire rings and developed recreation areas. For fire restriction details, visit firerestrictions.us/mt.

For further information, follow the Beaverhead-Deerlodge National Forest Facebook and Bitterroot National Forest Facebook pages.
