Community Meeting – West Fork Fire – Libby Christian Church – Monday, September 18th, 6 pm

Air support is available on both fires to assist suppression efforts as conditions allow. Structure assessment and protection measures have been implemented throughout both fire areas. Engine crews have been patrolling roaded fire perimeters overnight on both fires. Burnout operations are designed to create a low intensity, backing fire toward a containment point that fire managers can most safely and efficiently contain an active fire.

Weather: Cloudy skies with scattered showers will dominate the general area this week. Today, temperatures are only expected to reach the mid-50s with southwest winds 10-20 mph with minimum RH’s around 45%. Rain accumulations of 0.25 to 0.50 inches are possible.


West Fork Fire Yesterday: Fire managers increased containment to 33% that include areas of lower Quartz Creek and Bobtail Creek. Hotshot crews have completed hand burnout on the northeast flank east of Mt. Tom with a backing fire toward the Pipe Creek road. Firefighters patrolled and mopped up on the edges of the burnout in the Bull Creek/ Bobtail vicinity. All needed fuel break construction has been completed along Pipe Creek road.


Today’s Planned Actions: With the approaching wet weather, firefighters will concentrate on patrolling, holding, and improving all containment lines. The spot fire near Gold Hill will be monitored. Some rehabilitation of dozer lines around private property has started.

West Fork Fire Evacuations: Some evacuation orders have been lifted. Please check the Lincoln County Sheriff’s Facebook page or call (406) 293-6295 for the most up-to-date information on evacuations.

West Fork Fire Closures: The Kootenai National Forest has issued a closure order in effect for the West Fork Fire. The closure order and map are located under the closure tab on the Inciweb page.

_______________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________

Moose Peak Yesterday: Fire managers established containment of 11% that includes the northwest flank area around Miller Lake. Hotshot crews patrolled hand and dozer lines in East Fisher Creek on the north flank. The large aerial burnout operation on the northeast flank in the vicinity of McGinnis Meadows was patrolled and minor mop-up work along the edges was done A swing shift detected a spot fire about 2 acres in size and had it contained by 3 a.m. this morning. Mechanical fuel breaks were also extended southeast into the Fishtrap drainage.

Today’s Planned Actions: With the approaching wet weather, firefighters will further extend or hold and improve all hand and dozer lines and mechanized fuel breaks in the East Fisher Creek, McGinnis Creek and Fishtrap Creek areas. Saturday’s burnout will continue to be patrolled for containment issues.

The Deep Creek Fire located a few miles to the southwest of the Moose Peak Fire is being monitored by aerial resources.

Moose Peak Fire Pre - Evacuations: Pre-Evacuation notices have been issued for East Fisher Road, Bayhorse Ranch and McGinnis Meadows. Please check the Lincoln County Sheriff’s Facebook page or call (406) 293-6295 for the most up-to-date information on evacuations.

Moose Peak Fire Closures: The Kootenai National Forest has issued a closure order in effect for the Moose Peak Fire area. Sylvan Lake campground has been closed. The closure order and map are located under the closure tab on the Inciweb Page.
