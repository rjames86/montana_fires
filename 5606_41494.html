Revised Closure Order

Incident: Blacktail Fire Wildfire
Released: 30 min. ago

HELENA-LEWIS AND CLARK NATIONAL FOREST

Belt Creek-White Sulphur Springs Ranger District White Sulphur Springs, Montana

Order No. 01-15-14-17-56 EMERGENCY ORDER

National Forest System Road, Trails and Area Closure

Pursuant to 36 CFR 261.50(a) and (b), when provided by an order, the following acts are prohibited on the Belt Creek-White Sulphur Springs Ranger District, Helena-Lewis and Clark National Forest:

Being on the road. (36 CFR 261.54e) Being on the trail. (36 CFR 261.55a)

Going into or being upon an area – see map. (36 CFR 261.52e)

This area closure boundary includes all National Forest Service lands, roads and trails, including those listed below on the Belt Creek-White Sulphur Springs Ranger District (map). The area closure boundary will run from Buzzard Creek on the north to the Divide of Blacktail Creek and Middle Fork Big Elk Creek, then south to Lebo Fork and then due east three miles to the Forest Boundary at O’Hearn Creek, as shown on the attached map.

Closed roads include:

· National Forest System Road 654 in Wheatland County, T5N, R11E

Closed trails include:

· #630 – portion south of Buzzard Creek

· #630A – portion north of Lebo Fork of Big Elk Creek

· #640 – From Section 1 on the west side to junction of Trail 654 Elk Lake

· #654 – Elk Lake Trail

These restrictions are in support of the Blacktail Fire operations in the Crazy Mountains and will remain in effect until December 1st or until rescinded.

Pursuant to 36 CFR 261.50(e), the following persons are exempt from this Order:

1. Persons with a permit specifically authorizing the otherwise prohibited act or omission.
2. Any Federal, State, or Local Law Enforcement Officer or member of an organized rescue or firefighting force in the performance of an official duty.


These prohibitions are in addition to the general prohibitions in 36 CFR 261, Subpart A and a copy of this order will posted according to 36 CFR 261.51.

Executed in Helena, Montana, this _19th day of September 2017.

/s/William Avey

WILLIAM AVEY

Forest Supervisor

Violation of these prohibitions is punishable by a fine of not more than $5,000 for an individual or $10,000 for an organization, imprisonment for not more than 6 months, or both. (16 U.S.C. 551 and 18 U.S.C. 3559 and 3571).
