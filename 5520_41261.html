Crucifixion Creek, Strawberry & Scalp Fires
Rocky Mountain Ranger District
Saturday, September 16, 2017



Contact: Rocky Mountain Ranger District, 406-466-5341

Media Contact: Tom Story, 602-549-4094

Location: CRUCIFIXION CREEK Approximately 10 miles west of Heart Butte, in the Badger-Two Medicine area; STRAWBERRY —Eastern edge of fire is approximately ½ mile east of Swift Dam and extends south to Sheep Creek on the state land, 17 miles west of Dupuyer; SCALP—Approximately 7 miles west of Teton Pass Ski Area.

Fire Management: Winter storms continue to bring moisture to the fire, further reducing fire activity. Our remote weather stations indicate moisture levels on the fires to date are from between one tenth to one quarter inch of precipitation. A slow, consistent event is the most effective in cooling fire areas. While all area closures remain in place, evaluations are ongoing to adjust the closures in the near future. The fire is being managed by a Type 3 Incident Command Team. Crews are working on equipment refurbishment in preparation should fire activity pick up again. Crews and equipment operators will limit travel to and from the fire area to avoid unnecessary exposure to hazards created with the moisture and lessen the impact of heavy truck traffic on muddy roads. Improving weather conditions may allow aerial reconnaissance flights tomorrow. Updated GIS acreages are provided for each fire below.

Crucifixion Creek Fire Update (11,003 Acres): No new activity to report.

Strawberry Fire Update (30,461 Acres): No new activity to report.

Scalp Fire Update (20,406 Acres): No new activity to report.

Heart Butte Air Quality: Air quality across the Rocky Mountain Front, to include Heart Butte is forecasted as "Good" today. A smoke monitor is in place at Heart Butte, and information can be accessed through this site: https://wrcc.dri.edu/cgi-bin/smoke.pl. Click on the red location bubble that is numbered #3015 for information on the Heart Butte area. This data is time delayed by 6 hours. Plans are to remove the smoke monitor after this weekend.

Weather: Widespread rain and snow will gradually diminish through the day today. West to southwest winds will increase, especially along the Rocky Mountain Front for tonight and Sunday. Temperatures and humidity will also trend much warmer and drier Sunday than in the last few days, but only nearing more seasonable levels. A cooler and wetter pattern looks to continue through the next week as a series of disturbances looks poised to move across our region. However, forecasts are for less moisture than earlier predictions.

Public Meeting: Wednesday, September 20, 2017 at 6 p.m. in the Heart Butte School-Multi-Purpose Room

Additional Information: A PUBLIC AUTOMATED INFORMATION LINE was established for Alice Creek, Park Creek, Crucifixion Creek, Strawberry, Scalp, & Rice Ridge Fires. This number is (406) 495-3767. The Bob Marshall Wilderness Complex and the Helena-Lewis and Clark National Forest remain in Stage 1 fire restrictions.
