Meyers Fire Update September 6th, 2017

Incident: Meyers Fire Wildfire
Released: 20 min. ago

Related Information

  • Printable Meyers Fire Update September 6th, 2017 (pdf 95 kb)

Current Size: 56,333 acres Resources Assigned: 345

Status: The fire is burning in mixed conifer stands, primarily lodgepole pine, Douglas fir, and subalpine fir, with numerous standing dead trees. There are residences still at risk in the Moose Lake, Frog Pond, Springer Memorial Park and Bonanza Lands areas.

Yesterday’s Activities: The significant change in fire weather conditions the past two days gave firefighters a chance to reassess and adress the fire growth that occurred over the holiday weekend. The crews working at Moose Lake had the time and space to safely reset sprinkler systems throughout the community and perform burnout operations west of the Mooselake Rd. Around the Springer Memorial Park, dozer and hand line construction will provide a fire control line while sprinkler systems and other structure protection measures are implemented around buildings within the park. South of Frog Pond western extension of the fire front was assessed for its potential to impact the area from a new direction. Crews continued to prepare cabins to resist the fire’s approach. On the north end of the fire, burning conditions were not conducive to black line or burnout operations. Instead, crews used direct attack in the bottom of Senate Creek to contain a small spot fire.

Current Resources: Four 20-person crews, twenty-one engines, four pieces of heavy equipment (skidgines, dozers, masticators), ten water tenders, and three helicopters. Total personnel: 345.

Today’s Activities: With a warming trend and west/northwest winds returning to the Meyers Fire area, increases in fire activity can be expected. Firefighters will utilize conditions to their advantage to complete burnout operations east of Zeke’s meadow, south and west of Moose Lake, and in advantageous places within the Frog Pond area. Point protection of houses will continue as these tactics are implemented and in anticipation of expected fire advance. Bull dozer and hand line construction around Springer Memorial Park and Bonanza is ongoing as structure assessments and preparations are made amidst the communities. With the fire’s advance southeast across the Continental Divide into the upper portions of Howell’s Creek, firefighters are making aerial and ground observations to determine a course of action for point protection of values at risk. Mike Dueitt’s Southern Area Type 1 Incident Management Team will be arriving in Montana and familiarizing themselves with the Meyers Fire and Sapphire Complex. Management and command of the fires will transition to the new team on Thursday September 7, 2017 at 6:00PM.

Evacuations: The Frog Pond and Moose Lake areas are under an evacuation order. Additionally, an evacuation warning is in effect on East Fork Rd from FS Rd 5778 to Little East Fork Rd (FS RD 724) on the Bitterroot National Forest. Those under an evacuation warning should be prepared to leave at a moment’s notice.

Restrictions/Closures: Closure orders are in place on both National Forests. Bitterroot National Forest emergency closure orders are in effect for upper areas of the East Fork Bitterroot River. Beaverhead-Deerlodge National Forest has an emergency closure order around the fire extending into the Anaconda Pintler Wilderness. A temporary flight restriction (TFR) is in effect surrounding the Meyers Fire area. Granite County is under Stage 2 fire restrictions for private lands in the county. Amended Stage 1 fire restrictions are in effect today which prohibits ALL campfires in southwestern Montana on public lands, including in fire rings and developed recreation areas. For fire restriction details, visit firerestrictions.us/mt.

For further information, follow the Beaverhead-Deerlodge National Forest Facebook and Bitterroot National Forest Facebook pages.
