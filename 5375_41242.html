Ravalli County Evacuation Warnings Rescinded for Lolo Peak Fire

Incident: Lolo Peak Fire Wildfire
Released: 35 min. ago

Press Release Date :09/16/2017 11:15 AM

Ravalli County Sheriff Steve Holton said the remaining Evacuation Warnings due to the Lolo Peak Fire west of Hwy 93 from South Kootenai Creek Road to Holloway Lane have been rescinded, as a result of recent precipitation and the forecast for similar weather to continue next week.

The Evacuation Warnings on East Fork Road between Teepee Creek and Little East Fork were lifted yesterday. There are now no Evacuation Warnings or Orders in effect in Ravalli County due to fires.

Sheriff Holton said some forest closures remain in effect on Bass Creek and Sweeney Creek but residential properties are not affected. Sheriff Holton stated, "the Ravalli County Sheriff's Office is very happy that all of the warnings have been lifted and residents can safely re-populate all of their homes. We sincerely thank the fire fighters under the direction of Doug Turman's Northern Rockies Type 1 Incident Management Team and Unified Command on Lolo Peak, and the Southeast Type 1 Red Team on the Meyers Fire, as well as the Bitterroot National Forest Type 3 Team on Nelson Creek for their work to keep our friends and families safe from these fires. We also thank all of our citizens for their work, patience and understanding." Sheriff Holton continued, "we understand the circumstances and stress these incidents create for residents, and need to acknowledge all of the work land and home owners did to make their property defensible over the course of the summer. We also realize there is much work to do, as we move forward and continue to support each other over the coming months."

Sheriff Holton said the RCSO and Ravalli County Office of Emergency Management will continue to work with the Florence Volunteer Fire Department, Ravalli County Fire Warden and the in-coming Type 2 Incident Management Team assigned to the Lolo Peak Fire.

Summary :The remaining Evacuation Warnings due to the Lolo Peak Fire have been rescinded effective 11 a.m. today. There are now no evacuation warnings or orders in Ravalli County.Press Release Prepared by :Sheriff Steve Holton
