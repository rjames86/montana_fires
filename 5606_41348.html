Blacktail Fire Update 9-18-17

Incident: Blacktail Fire Wildfire
Released: 18 min. ago

Fire Information Line: (406) 333-1544

Follow Fire Information on Inciweb: https://inciweb.nwcg.gov/incident/5606/

Email: blacktailfire2017@gmail.com

Important – Drones are prohibited in the fires vicinity & have potential to shut down all operations.

Start date: September 10, 2017
Location: Southwest of Two Dot, Montana
Cause: Lightning Size: 5,351
Containment: 25%
Total Personnel: 101 personnel

Yesterday’s Operations: Crews worked from pre-identified drop points, or staging areas, assessing conditions and determining where they may have access to the fire area. Because of the rain and snow received over the previous few days, entry into much of the fire area has been very difficult. Members of the Incident Command Team were able to fly over the fire yesterday to further assess the status of the fire and area conditions. The Incident Command Team and agency administrators held two community meetings in Melville and Two Dot.

Planned Operations: Due to current conditions and expected weather, a substantial number of the fire’s resources will be demobilized today and available for other incidents. The remaining personnel will patrol and monitor the fire. Another reconnaissance flight is scheduled for today.

Closures: Helena-Lewis and Clark National Forest closures for the fire area (Order No. 01-15-07-17-45 and Order No. 01-15-07-17-47) were rescinded Saturday morning. Listings of the roads and trails now open are posted on the fire’s Inciweb closure link. However, an area closure is still in effect for within the fire’s perimeter.

Resources Assigned: Cain’s Nevada Interagency Type III Incident Management Team is still assigned to the fire. The team will transfer operational control of the fire to the local unit on Wednesday morning. One dozer, one grader, an engine, and a Type 3 helicopter are currently assigned to the incident.

Weather: The forecast calls for partly sunny skies on Monday, with a cold front approaching late in the afternoon. The front is expected to bring gusty winds and a chance of showers. A mix of rain and snow is possible Monday night into Tuesday.

Fire History: The fire was reported at approximately 3:00 pm on Sunday, September 10. Nine smokejumpers initial attacked the fire and were assisted by local resources, including volunteer fire departments from Two Dot, Melville, Wheatland, and Sweet Grass counties. Firefighting efforts were assisted with two helicopters and single engine air tankers dropping retardant as needed. Suppression efforts were hampered by high winds coupled with steep rocky terrain. This fire includes both Federal and Private Lands in Meagher, Wheatland, and Sweet Grass Counties.
