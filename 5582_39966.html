Fire Update 9-4-17

Incident: East Fork Fire Wildfire
Released: 25 min. ago

A public meeting for Hill County has been set for 4 p.m. on
Monday, September 4th at the Havre High School.

Background: The East Fork Fire started on August 27, 2017, the cause is under investigation. It is on private lands in Blaine County, Hill County and on the Rocky Boy Reservation, south of Havre, Montana. A small piece of Bureau of Land Management land is also within the fire area. Volunteer, county, state and federal firefighters from Blaine and Hill Counties did an outstanding job of coming together to conduct an aggressive initial attach on this fire. As firefighting moves into the next phases, many of these firefighters will continue to make significant contributions to the suppression of the fire.

Yesterday: Yesterday’s predicted the cold front moved through the area, however winds remained light. Fire behavior was minimal allowing firefighters to identify and extinguish areas of heat. Mop up continued along the fire edge and where secured, the lines were declared contained.

Actions Planned: Today’s focus remains securing the fire the perimeter, and identifying and extinguishing any areas of heat. Patrol, mop up and suppression rehabilitation will begin along the fire’s perimeter as conditions and staffing allow. In Hill County the fire is roughly bordered by Beaver Creek on the west and Sucker Creek Road on the north. In Blaine County the fire is west of the Clear Creek Road. To the South the fire is roughly bordered by the west of fork of Clear Creek.

Evacuations and Closures: Beaver Creek Road is open from 5th Avenue through to Taylor Road and to Rocky Boy Agency. Beaver Creek Park is closed to recreation, and the Hill County Sheriff’s Department is asking the public to stay out of the area for firefighter safety- the side roads within the park are still closed to the public. Sucker Creek Road is closed between Beaver Creek and Clear Creek.

Announcements: A public meeting for Hill County has been set for 4 p.m. on Monday, September 4th at the Havre High School auditorium. Upcoming public meetings will be announced soon for Blaine County and the Chippewa Cree Tribe; stay tuned for further information. The meetings will be facilitated by the Incident Management Team to help the local jurisdiction share information with area residents. Members of the Incident Team will give an update on the current fire situation and expected suppression activities over the next few days. County representatives will be on hand to talk about the fire from their perspective and about plans for the future.

Weather: A Red Flag Warning remains in effect for today. However, winds will be expected to moderate and be out of the north at 10 to 15 mph, occasionally gusting to 20 mph. Temperatures will be in the high 60’s to low 70s.
