Sapphire Complex Update for September 9, 2017

Incident: Sapphire Complex Wildfire
Released: 5 min. ago

The Southern Area Type I Incident Management Team, with Incident Commander Mike Dueitt, has assumed command of both the Sapphire Complex and the Meyers Fire. The contact phone number for both fires is 406-859-9107, and the Incident Command Post is now at the Meyers Fire camp off Skalkaho Road.

A Red Flag Warning will be in effect today from 2:00pm to 9:00pm. Gusty winds may significantly increase fire behavior and create hazards from falling trees on both the Sapphire Complex and Meyers Fires. Structure protection will be the main focus today. The small amount of rain yesterday had a very minimal impact on fire behavior, especially with the winds moving through the area today.


Sapphire Complex: 43,516 acres Location: Approximately 25 miles south-southeast of Missoula

Containment: 53% Cause: Lightning Total Personnel: 304

Three fires comprise the Sapphire Complex: Little Hogback, Goat Creek, and Sliderock. The fires are burning in steep, rocky terrain between Rock Creek and Upper Willow Creek drainages.

Little Hogback Fire: 34,310 acres, 30% contained.

Fire activity was minimal along the entire perimeter with only a few interior hot spots. Crews will continue monitoring handlines in Windlass Gulch, and mopping up on the east and north sides of the fire. Structure protection along Rock Creek Road remains a priority for firefighters, and protection of life is always the primary objective.

Goat Creek Fire: 8,305 acres, 95% contained.

Crews are performing suppression repair, including piling and sorting timber, where practical. Rehabilitation of firelines and suppression activities continues.

Sliderock Fire: 901 acres, 95% contained.

Crews continue to mop up hotspots and patrol the Sliderock Fire.


Meyers Fire: 59,933 acres Location: Approximately 25 miles southwest of Philipsburg, Montana

Containment: 0% Cause: Lightning Total Personnel: 350

The fire is burning in mixed conifer stands, primarily lodgepole pine, Douglas-fir, and subalpine fir, with many standing dead trees. There are residences still at risk including those in the Moose Lake, Frog Pond, Billy Springer Memorial Park and Bonanza Lands areas.


Yesterday’s Activities: Fire activity was minimal across the Meyers Fire. Crews continued mop-up across the fire including areas around Moose Lake, Zeke’s Meadows and on the northwest flank of the fire. Structure protection is in place in the East Fork of the Bitterroot with contingency lines being built around the Billy Springer Memorial and Bonanza subdivisions as well as other private properties. A public meeting was held last night for the East Fork Bitterroot communities with approximately 65 people in attendance.


Today’s Activities: Structure protection continues to be the priority on the fire. Hand and mechanical line improvement measures will continue in the Billy Springer Memorial and Bonanza subdivisions as well as other private properties in the area. Firefighters will continue mopping up across the fire area and identify opportunities for possible direct hand lines around Zeke’s Meadow to the west. Crews will also be scouting opportunities to improve contingency lines to reduce impacts and threats.

Resources Threatened

Sapphire Complex: Private property and structures in the Rock Creek, Upper Willow Creek, and Marshall Creek drainages remain threatened. Other resources threatened include fisheries in Rock Creek, grazing lands along the southern boundary of the Little Hogback Fire, Bonneville Power Administration powerline, and a communication site.

Meyers Fire: Private property and structures in the Moose Lake, Frog Pond, Billy Springer Memorial Park, Bonanza Lake areas, as well as other scattered individual dwellings.

Evacuations

Sapphire Complex: A mandatory evacuation (Level Red) order for approximately thirty-five residences along Rock Creek Road remains in effect, beginning from and including Wild Rose Loop south to Stony Creek. All other pre-evacuation (Level Yellow) notices are still in effect. For information on evacuation planning refer to the Ready-Set-Go materials available at http://www.wildlandfirersg.org/Portals/18/Resources/States/MT/RSG_Montana_ActionGuide_web.pdf

Meyers Fire: The Frog Pond and Moose Lake areas are under an evacuation order. Additionally, an evacuation warning is in effect on East Fork Rd from FS Rd 5778 to Little East Fork Rd (FS RD 724) on the Bitterroot National Forest. Those under an evacuation warning should be prepared to leave at a moment’s notice.

Closures

Sapphire Complex: The Rock Creek Road from I-90 south to Bitterroot Campground (MP 23.5) is OPEN. Due to fire traffic congestion and fire safety concerns, Rock Creek Road will stay CLOSED from Bitterroot Campground, south to Cornish Gulch (FSR5027), and public traffic is NOT allowed. Upper Willow Creek Road is currently closed to the public. The Beaverhead-Deerlodge National Forest reduced closure restrictions in the Black Pine ridge area, but maintains all area closures in Upper Willow Creek, North Fork Lower Willow Creek, and Harvey Creek areas. The Bureau of Land Management has an area closure in Upper Willow Creek, Ram Mountain, and nearby areas. The Lolo and Beaverhead-Deerlodge National Forests, the Bureau of Land Management, and the Montana Department of Natural Resources (DNRC) closure orders and maps are posted under the closures tab on Inciweb. A temporary flight restriction (TFR) remains in effect surrounding the Little Hogback Fire area, but has been rescinded over the Goat Creek and Sliderock fire areas.

Meyers Fire: Closure orders are in place in both the Bitterroot and Beaverhead-Deerlodge National Forests. Bitterroot National Forest emergency closure orders are in effect for upper areas of the East Fork Bitterroot River. The expanded Beaverhead-Deerlodge National Forest closure is still in effect. A temporary flight restriction (TFR) remains in effect surrounding the Meyers Fire area. Granite County is under Stage 2 fire restrictions for private lands in the county. Amended Stage 1 fire restrictions are in effect today which prohibits ALL campfires in southwestern Montana on public lands, including in fire rings and developed recreation areas. For fire restriction details, visit firerestrictions.us/mt.

Please adhere to all closures and allow our firefighters to perform their jobs safely.


Weather

Dense smoke moderated temperatures again yesterday, with highs only in the mid-80s in the valleys and the mid- to upper 70s on ridgetops. Humidities continued to be in the teens with light, terrain driven winds. Thunderstorms last night brought a small amount of rain to the fire areas, but no significant amounts. Today, winds are expected to pick up as low pressure moves into the area.


For further information, follow the Beaverhead-Deerlodge National Forest Facebook, Bitterroot National Forest Facebook or Lolo National Forest pages.
