Meyers Sapphire Complex Fires Update Saturday, September 9, 2017

Incident: Sapphire Complex Wildfire
Released: 32 min. ago

Fire Information Line: 406-859-9107Email: meyerswhetstone@gmail.comInciweb: https://inciweb.nwcg.gov/incident/5364/https://inciweb.nwcg.gov/incident/5397/
Follow Fire Information on Facebook: Lolo National Forest Beaverhead-Deerlodge National ForestBitterroot National Forest
The Southern Area Type I Incident Management Team, with Incident Commander Mike Dueitt, has assumed command ofboth the Sapphire Complex and the Meyers Fire. The contact phone number for both fires is 406-859-9107, and the IncidentCommand Post is now at the Meyers Fire camp off Skalkaho Road.
A Red Flag Warning will be in effect today from 2:00pm to 9:00pm. Gusty winds may significantly increase firebehavior and create hazards from falling trees on both the Sapphire Complex and Meyers Fires. Structure protectionwill be the main focus today. The small amount of rain yesterday had a very minimal impact on fire behavior,especially with the winds moving through the area today.
Sapphire Complex: 43,516 acres Location: Approximately 25 miles south-southeast of MissoulaContainment: 53% Cause: Lightning Total Personnel: 304Three fires comprise the Sapphire Complex: Little Hogback, Goat Creek, and Sliderock. The fires are burning in steep, rockyterrain between Rock Creek and Upper Willow Creek drainages.
Little Hogback Fire: 34,310 acres, 30% contained.Fire activity was minimal along the entire perimeter with only a few interior hot spots. Crews will continuemonitoring handlines in Windlass Gulch, and mopping up on the east and north sides of the fire. Structure protectionalong Rock Creek Road remains a priority for firefighters, and protection of life is always the primary objective.
Goat Creek Fire: 8,305 acres, 95% contained.Crews are performing suppression repair, including piling and sorting timber, where practical. Rehabilitation offirelines and suppression activities continues.
Sliderock Fire: 901 acres, 95% contained.Crews continue to mop up hotspots and patrol the Sliderock Fire.
Meyers Fire: 59,933 acres Location: Approximately 25 miles southwest of Philipsburg, MontanaContainment: 0% Cause: Lightning Total Personnel: 350The fire is burning in mixed conifer stands, primarily lodgepole pine, Douglas-fir, and subalpine fir, with many standing deadtrees. There are residences still at risk including those in the Moose Lake, Frog Pond, Billy Springer Memorial Park andBonanza Lands areas.
Yesterday’s Activities: Fire activity was minimal across the Meyers Fire. Crews continued mop-up across the fire includingareas around Moose Lake, Zeke’s Meadows and on the northwest flank of the fire. Structure protection is in place in the EastFork of the Bitterroot with contingency lines being built around the Billy Springer Memorial and Bonanza subdivisions aswell as other private properties. A public meeting was held last night for the East Fork Bitterroot communities withapproximately 65 people in attendance.
Today’s Activities: Structure protection continues to be the priority on the fire. Hand and mechanical line improvementmeasures will continue in the Billy Springer Memorial and Bonanza subdivisions as well as other private properties in thearea. Firefighters will continue mopping up across the fire area and identify opportunities for possible direct hand linesaround Zeke’s Meadow to the west. Crews will also be scouting opportunities to improve contingency lines to reduce impactsand threats.
Resources ThreatenedSapphire Complex: Private property and structures in the Rock Creek, Upper Willow Creek, and Marshall Creek drainagesremain threatened. Other resources threatened include fisheries in Rock Creek, grazing lands along the southern boundary ofthe Little Hogback Fire, Bonneville Power Administration powerline, and a communication site.Meyers Fire: Private property and structures in the Moose Lake, Frog Pond, Billy Springer Memorial Park, Bonanza Lakeareas, as well as other scattered individual dwellings.
EvacuationsSapphire Complex: A mandatory evacuation (Level Red) order for approximately thirty-five residences along Rock CreekRoad remains in effect, beginning from and including Wild Rose Loop south to Stony Creek. All other pre-evacuation (LevelYellow) notices are still in effect. For information on evacuation planning refer to the Ready-Set-Go materials available athttp://www.wildlandfirersg.org/Portals/18/Resources/States/MT/RSG_Montana_ActionGuide_web.pdfMeyers Fire: The Frog Pond and Moose Lake areas are under an evacuation order. Additionally, an evacuation warning is ineffect on East Fork Rd from FS Rd 5778 to Little East Fork Rd (FS RD 724) on the Bitterroot National Forest. Those underan evacuation warning should be prepared to leave at a moment’s notice.
ClosuresSapphire Complex: The Rock Creek Road from I-90 south to Bitterroot Campground (MP 23.5) is OPEN. Due to fire trafficcongestion and fire safety concerns, Rock Creek Road will stay CLOSED from Bitterroot Campground, south to CornishGulch (FSR5027), and public traffic is NOT allowed. Upper Willow Creek Road is currently closed to the public. TheBeaverhead-Deerlodge National Forest reduced closure restrictions in the Black Pine ridge area, but maintains all areaclosures in Upper Willow Creek, North Fork Lower Willow Creek, and Harvey Creek areas. The Bureau of LandManagement has an area closure in Upper Willow Creek, Ram Mountain, and nearby areas. The Lolo and Beaverhead-Deerlodge National Forests, the Bureau of Land Management, and the Montana Department of Natural Resources (DNRC)closure orders and maps are posted under the closures tab on Inciweb. A temporary flight restriction (TFR) remains in effectsurrounding the Little Hogback Fire area, but has been rescinded over the Goat Creek and Sliderock fire areas.Meyers Fire: Closure orders are in place in both the Bitterroot and Beaverhead-Deerlodge National Forests. BitterrootNational Forest emergency closure orders are in effect for upper areas of the East Fork Bitterroot River. The expandedBeaverhead-Deerlodge National Forest closure is still in effect. A temporary flight restriction (TFR) remains in effectsurrounding the Meyers Fire area. Granite County is under Stage 2 fire restrictions for private lands in the county. AmendedStage 1 fire restrictions are in effect today which prohibits ALL campfires in southwestern Montana on public lands,including in fire rings and developed recreation areas. For fire restriction details, visit firerestrictions.us/mt.Please adhere to all closures and allow our firefighters to perform their jobs safely.
WeatherDense smoke moderated temperatures again yesterday, with highs only in the mid-80s in the valleys and the mid- to upper70s on ridgetops. Humidities continued to be in the teens with light, terrain driven winds. Thunderstorms last night brought asmall amount of rain to the fire areas, but no significant amounts. Today, winds are expected to pick up as low pressuremoves into the area.
For further information, visit https://inciweb.nwcg.gov and select “Meyers Fire” or “Sapphire Complex” in Montana, andfollow the Beaverhead-Deerlodge National Forest Facebook, Bitterroot National Forest Facebook or Lolo National Forestpages.
