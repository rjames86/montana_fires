SARTIN DRAW FIRE UPDATE

MT DNRC COUNTY ASSIST TEAM

David Hamilton, Incident Commander

SIZE: 99,735 acres

CONTAINMENT: 85%
CAUSE: Natural
FIRE LOCATION: 20 miles NE of Ashland, MT

RESOURCES:
20 Engines
5 Crews (20 people/crew, 4 - Type 2 Crews & 1 - Type 1 Crew)

11 Heavy Equipment (5 dozers, 3 skidgines, 4 water tender)

1 Helicopter

227 Total Personnel


Weather: A Red Flag Warning will be in effect until 9 PM tonight. Winds will shift back towards the north this morning and will again be 10-20 mph during the afternoon, with gusts up to 25 mph. A Canadian cold front passed through the area early this morning, producing much cooler temperatures, higher humidities, and gusty north winds. Cool temperatures will continue through Tuesday, followed by a warming trend on Wednesday and Thursday.

Fire Situation: Fire resources will mop-up and secure 200 feet in along the fires perimeter to increase containment. Perform rehab on all interior dozer lines and where appropriate, rehab perimeter dozer lines. Pockets of unburned fuel (i.e. vegetation) in the deep interior of the fire may burn and produce smoke. There will be two 20 person crews, two engines, one water tender, and one skidgine working on the Snider Branch portion of the fire. They will be securing the perimeter and mopping up.

Yesterday, firefighters along with local ranchers accomplished mopping up hot spots 3 chains (200 feet) in on most areas of the fire. Their hard work increased the containment on the Sartin Draw and Snider Fires to 85%. Firelines held throughout the day with the windy hot conditions. The increase in fire size is due to the Sartin Draw and Snider fires being combined.

On Saturday, September 2, the County Assist Team also assumed management of the Snider Fire (6,393 acres) burning in Rosebud County. Thursday morning the two fires burned together along a small section of the Tongue River Road. The Snider fire was lightning caused and first reported on August 31. The fire is located 16 miles east of Colstrip. Rosebud Fire Warden, local ranchers and heavy equipment worked hard and did a good job slowing the progress of the fire.

Stage I Fire Restrictions are in Effect in Eastern Montana.

CAMPFIRES and other fires are prohibited except in approved recreation sites, as designated on the fire prevention order. Liquid petroleum and LPG stoves that can be turned on and off are allowed.

SMOKING is prohibited outside of vehicles, buildings, and developed recreation sites, unless you are in a 3-foot-diameter area cleared of all burnable vegetation.

For more information about fire restrictions, go to: http://firerestrictions.us
