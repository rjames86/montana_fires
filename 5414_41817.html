Rice Ridge Fire Update - September 26th, 2017

Incident: Rice Ridge Fire Wildfire
Released: 15 min. ago

Related Information

  • Printable Update for September 26th, 2017 (pdf 254 kb)


Fire origin: July 24, 2017, 1.4 miles northeast of Seeley Lake Montana.
Fire Information: (406) 813-0527 Hours: 8am – 8pm
Email: RiceRidgeFire@gmail.com
Facebook: Lolo National Forest Facebook Page
 Jurisdiction: Lolo National Forest & Montana Department of Natural Resources and Conservation.

Community meeting in Ovando tonight, Tuesday, September 26th at 6pm, Ovando Elementary School
Closures: Road, trail and area closures are still in effect. Local Forest managers, in coordination with fire operations personnel, are evaluating the potential to modify area closures daily. Public should respect closures due to heavy equipment working in the area and the hazards associated with weakened trees and snags. Visit the Lolo and Flathead National Forests webpages for more information on closures on National Forests lands. Visit fwp.mt.gov or call 406-542-5500 for up-to-date information on Fish, Wildlife and Park land and hunting closures.

Weather forecast: Cloudy skies will prevail over the area today, although the upper level disturbance may continue to bring a few light rain showers through the morning. Warming and drying conditions are expected to build through the later part of the week which may lead to increased fire behavior in isolated islands of unburned fuels interior to the control lines. Wet weather is projected to return over the weekend which will reduce fire activity.


Activities: Despite the cool, damp weather, heat still persists in portions of the fire area. Crews are concentrating mop up near Pyramid Pass trailhead, in the vicinity of the Monture Guard Station and nearby trails, and in the swamps to extinguish smoldering heat. They will continue to patrol fire lines and haul back sprinklers and other water handling equipment no longer needed. Suppression repair is the other big job facing the firefighters. It is a long slow process but there has been good progress in repairing road surfaces and providing erosion barriers which were disrupted during fire activities, chipping slash and piling logs resulting from the creation of shaded fuel breaks, and returning roads and trails to a condition suitable for public use. Repair work today is focusing on many of the secondary roads and interior firelines. Fire managers, in coordination with local resource advisors, continue to assess the fire area to refine the areas needing repair work and timeframes that are appropriate for doing the work. While the miles in need of repair is expected to change, about 150 miles has been identified; of that, 45 miles of the work is completed.


Completed·: The suppression strategy for this fire is full suppression on 23% of the fire perimeter and confinement on 12% of the fire perimeter for a total of 35%. The remaining 65% of the fire perimeter is located in remote backcountry and wilderness and will be monitored for fire activity as fall weather arrives. Today’s “Completed” percentage in the summary table above indicates that 72% of the full suppression/confinement actions taken on the fire have been completed.
