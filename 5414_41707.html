Rice Ridge Fire Update - September 23,2017

Incident: Rice Ridge Fire Wildfire
Released: 45 min. ago

Related Information

  • September 23rd Update for the Rice Ridge Fire (pdf 112 kb)


Fire origin: July 24, 2017, 1.4 miles northeast of Seeley Lake Montana.
Fire Information: (406) 813-0527 Hours: 8am – 8pm
Email: RiceRidgeFire@gmail.com
Inciweb: https://inciweb.nwcg.gov/incident/5414
Facebook: Lolo National Forest Facebook Page
Jurisdiction: Lolo National Forest & Montana Department of Natural Resources and Conservation.

Community meetings are scheduled as followed:
Seeley Lake - Monday, September 25th at 6pm, Mission Bible Fellowship Church
Ovando - Tuesday, September 26th at 6pm, Ovando Elementary School

Closures: Road, trail and area closures are still in effect. Local Forest managers, in coordination with fire operations personnel, are evaluating the potential to modify area closures daily. Public should obey closures due to heavy equipment working in the area and the hazards associated with weakened trees and snags. Visit the Lolo and Flathead National Forests webpages for more information for closures on National Forests lands. Visit fwp.mt.gov or call 406-542-5500 for up-to-date information on Fish, Wildlife and Park land and hunting closures.

Summary of yesterday’s actions: Heavy equipment and crews are making progress with the fire line suppression rehabilitation. Over the last two days, 25.5 miles of fire line has been repaired, inspected, and marked as complete. Firefighters performing mop-up and patrol of the most recent burnout found some residual heat in stumps and took action to extinguish the heat.

Today’s planned activities: Crews will continue chipping slash along the 720 Road. The sprinklers will continue to water the bog area between Blind Canyon and Swamp Creek. Fire line suppression rehabitation crews will be working old fire lines in the Nordic area. Mopping up will continue along the 477 Road in the Kozy Korner area. If weather is favorable, the helicopter assigned to the fire should be able to move from Missoula to Seeley Lake and will be available for reconnaissance flights.

Weather forecast: Today will start with patchy fog and cooler temperatures with a slight chance of light showers. Beginning Sunday, there will be a gradual warming trend through Monday.

Completed·: The suppression strategy for this fire is full suppression on 23% of the fire perimeter and confinement on 12% of the fire perimeter for a total of 35%. The remaining 65% of the fire perimeter is located in remote backcountry and wilderness and will be monitored for any activity as fall weather arrives. Today’s “Completed” percentage in the summary table above indicates that 72% of the full suppression/confinement actions taken on the fire have been completed.
