Sapphire Complex Daily Update 08.31.2017

Incident: Sapphire Complex Wildfire
Released: 2 days ago

Related Information

  • Sapphire Complex Daily Update 08.31.2017 (pdf 137 kb)

Three fires comprise the Sapphire Complex: Little Hogback, Goat Creek, and Sliderock fires. Mike Almas’ Northern Rockies Type II Incident Management Team (IMT) is managing the incident. The fires are burning in steep, rocky terrain between the Rock Creek and Upper Willow Creek drainages. Fire managers will hold a public meeting jointly for both the Sapphire Complex and the Meyers Fire TONIGHT at 5:30 p.m. at the Granite County High School library.

Little Hogback Fire: 30,929 acres, 30% contained.

Wednesday, the fire continued to back downhill along the southwestern flank between upper Sheep Gulch and Capron Creek, resulting in the evacuation of three structures in Capron Creek drainage, along Rock Creek Road. Fire managers continue to seek opportunities to complete indirect line and secure firelines along the southwest corner where fire intensity and treacherous terrain continue to hamper containment efforts. Firefighters may need to perform a small burnout operation in that area in order to protect structures. Elsewhere around the fire, previously unburned fuels within the fire’s interior continued to burn and torch. Crews made significant headway holding and further securing existing firelines, mopping up hot spots that started smoldering and smoking with the increased winds, while remaining vigilant in case spot fires were to occur. Structure protection measures are still in place along Rock Creek Road. Preventing fire growth toward homes and ranches continues to dictate operational priorities, and protection of life is always the primary objective.

Goat Creek Fire: 8,305 acres, 95% contained.

Limited suppression repair has begun, where practical, while crews continue to mop-up and patrol some portions of the fire.

Sliderock Fire: 901 acres, 90% contained.

Crews continue to mop up on hotspots around the Sliderock Fire perimeter and will continue to patrol the area.

Resources Threatened: Private property and structures in the Rock Creek, Upper Willow Creek, and Marshall Creek drainages remain threatened. Other resources threatened include fisheries in Rock Creek, grazing lands along the southern boundary of the Little Hogback Fire, Bonneville Power Administration powerline, and a communication site.

Evacuations: Late Wednesday, the Granite County Sheriff issued an evacuation order for three structures in the lower end of Capron Creek, near mile marker 33 on Rock Creek Road. Many homes and communities are still potentially threatened by the fires within the Sapphire Complex, and as a result “Yellow” Pre-evacuation status for those areas remain in effect. See Inciweb maps and evacuation alerts for details.

Closures: The Lolo National Forest instituted a new area closure, effective Thursday, August 31, which allows public access to the Bitterroot Campground. The Rock Creek Road from I-90 south to Bitterroot Campground (MP 23.5) is OPEN. Due to fire traffic congestion and fire safety concerns, the Rock Creek Road will continue to be CLOSED from Bitterroot Campground, south to Cornish Gulch, and public traffic is NOT allowed. Upper Willow Creek Road is currently closed to the public. The Beaverhead-Deerlodge National Forest has reduced closure restrictions in the Black Pine ridge area, but maintains all area closures in the Upper Willow Creek, North Fork Lower Willow Creek, and Harvey Creek areas. The Bureau of Land Management has implemented an area closure in the Upper Willow Creek, Ram Mountain, and nearby areas. The Lolo and Beaverhead-Deerlodge National Forests and the Bureau of Land Management have posted closure orders and maps under the closures tab on Inciweb.

Weather: Sapphire Complex meteorologists issued a Red Flag Warning again today! Wednesday’s thunderstorms resulted in gusty winds over portions of the fire area; however, measurable precipitation only fell on Goat Creek Fire.

Fire Restrictions: Stage 1 Fire Restrictions are in place on the Beaverhead-Deerlodge National Forest (Forest Service land east of Sandstone Ridge), with the additional modification to prohibit all campfires. Stage 2 Fire Restrictions are in place on BLM, private and state lands in Granite County, and the Lolo National Forest (Forest Service land west of Sandstone Ridge).

For information on fire restrictions, please visit firerestrictions.us.
