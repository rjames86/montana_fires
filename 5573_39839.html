Sunday, September 3, 2017 Morning Update


Weather: A Red Flag Warning is effect from 9 AM today to 9 PM Monday. Weather today will be hot and very dry. High temperatures will approach 100 degrees in spots and humidities will fall to around 10% in the afternoon. Northwest winds will increase by late morning, with expected gusts to 30 mph when the cold front passes through.

Sartin Draw Fire: Today fire resources will improve line, mop-up containment lines, and patrol portions of the fire. The main priority is to keep the fire in place through the winds today. Yesterday, firefighters in conjunction with local ranchers put in another hard day of work and made great progress in containing and suppressing the fire. There are containment lines (dozer constructed, black line and/or road systems) along the edge of the fire.

Snider Fire: On Saturday, September 2 at 12:00 PM, the County Assist Team also assumed management of the Snider Fire burning in Rosebud County. On Friday, September 1st the two fires burned together along a small section of the Tongue River Road. Aviation resources flew the fires yesterday and determined the fire is 6,393 acres in size. The Snider fire was reported around 12:00 PM on Thursday, August 31 and the cause is natural. The fire is 16 miles east of Colstrip, MT and started on private land. Rosebud fire warden, local ranchers and some heavy equipment worked hard for days to slow the progress of the fire. Currently a Type 1 Hot Shot crew is working the Snider Fire. The priority today is to secure the SE and S sides of the fire, continue to build and improve line, burn out areas of unburned line as conditions and resources allow and mop-up 200 feet in from the fire perimeter.


RESOURCES:
22 Engines
5 Crews (4 Type 2 Initial Attack 20 person crews and 1 Type 1 Hot Shot 20 person crew)
11 Heavy Equipment (5 dozers, 3 skidgines, 3 water tender)

212 Total Personnel
