Liberty Fire Update

Wednesday, September 6, 2017, 8:30 am Northern Rockies Incident Management Team Greg Poncin, Incident Commander


Fire Information: 406-272-4354, 8 am – 8 pm Email: LibertyFire2017@gmail.com

Websites: inciweb.nwcg.gov/incident/5437/#

Facebook: https://www.facebook.com/lolonationalforest/

Location: 17 miles SE of Arlee, Montana in the South Fork Primitive Area
Yesterday’s Accomplishments: Firefighters continued to secure fire line on the south and southeast edge of the fire and to burnout unburned islands of fuel to Belmont Road. Firefighters also conducted burnout operations in the Boles Creek Area to improve and secure work already completed in the northeast edge of the fire near the South Jocko Tribal Primitive Area and the 2007 Jocko Lakes fire. This provides for firefighter safety as it decreases the likelihood of the fire spotting below firefighters as they begin work along Finley Creek and Placid Creek roads.


Today’s Planned Actions: Firefighters will continue to monitor fire spread and complete indirect containment line. Firefighters on the south end of the fire will continue to construct, maintain, improve, patrol, and hold containment lines. Crews will also actively suppress spot fires, and mitigate hazard trees

near containment lines through the use of mechanized equipment and hand crews, to impede fires growth. Heavy equipment is being used near roads and other accessible areas to complete critical containment lines. Use of heavy equipment minimizes exposure and risk to firefighters in areas with numerous snags. As weather conditions allow, firing operations off current and constructed containment lines will continue on the east and south perimeter of the fire. On the north perimeter, crews will continue to check fire spread to hold fire south of the primary containment line and in Liberty Creek. Aircraft are being used when available to support checking fire growth and to control spots. Firefighters will continue to monitor areas where fire continues to burn in the Rattlesnake Wilderness and the South Jocko Tribal Primitive Area.


Weather and Fire Behavior: A warming and drying trend is expected to continue through Thursday. The atmosphere will also be stable today with smoke dispersion remaining problematic especially for the lower elevations. As the inversion breaks and the instability increases expect group tree torching and upslope crown runs during the peak burning period late in the afternoon and early evening. As fire activity increases, due to rising temperatures and lower relative humidity, there is a potential for a smoke and ember column to build above the fire perimeter and the possibility for spotting across containment lines increases.

Air Operations: Aircraft assigned to the fire are available for this incident and for initial attack. A temporary flight restriction is in effect over the fire area. Flying drones over the fire is prohibited. If drones are detected, air operations will stop.

Closures: An area closure is in place for public health and safety due to wildfire. The closure area begins on the Confederated Salish and Kootenai Tribes (CSKT) reservation Boundary line and continues east following the CSKT Boundary along the Middle Fork of Jocko River and Jocko Lake turning southwest; then heading East and southeast crossing Second Creek, Grouse Creek, Buck Creek and Finley Creek; then south to Boles Creek. Continuing due east to Vaughn Creek, then due south crossing North Fork Blanchard Creek, Cold Brook and Blanchard Creek; continuing west crossing Belmont Creek and Gold Creek then turning northwest crossing Spring Creek, West Fork Gold Creek, next to and including Boulder Lake, Wapiti Lake and Crazy Fish Lake; heading north on a straight line until it intersects with the point of beginning.

All trails and roads that are within, and those that make up the boundaries to this area closure, are closed due to the Liberty fire. The South Fork Primitive area of Confederated Salish and Kootenai Tribes is closed.

Evacuations: Currently, there are no evacuation warnings or orders

The fire is under the jurisdiction of Confederated Salish and Kootenai Tribes, Montana Department of Natural Resources (DNRC), and Lolo National Forest

Cooperating Agencies: Bureau of Land Management, Bureau of Indian Affairs, Lake County Office of Emergency Management, Lake County Office of Emergency Services, Missoula County and Seeley Lake Fire, and The Nature Conservancy
