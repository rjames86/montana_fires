Daily Update 9/18 Highway 200 Complex Fires

Incident: Highway 200 Complex Wildfire
Released: 27 min. ago

Approximate Size (acres/%containment):
Sheep Gap –24,816/40
Deep & Miller Creek –16,327/0

Cub Creek –5,823/11
Readers – 152/95
TOTAL – 47,118/18

Total personnel: 538

Location: The Highway 200 Complex fires are located in Sanders County, MT near the towns of Thompson Falls and Plains. The Sheep Gap fire is approximately 2 miles west of Plains. The remaining complex fires are located 8 miles north and west of Thompson Falls.

Evacuations and Closures: The Sanders County Sheriff's Office has lifted the evacuation notice for Swamp Creek Road from Four Corners to the intersection of the East and West fork of Swamp Creek. Pre-evacuation warnings are in place on Blackjack Rd. from Four Corners to where it intersects River Rd West and Swamp Creek Road from Four Corners to the intersection of the East and West fork of Swamp Creek. Other areas under pre-evacuation remain so. Members of the Montana Army National Guard are assisting with road closures, pre-evacuations, and any other assistance that may be needed. The Forest Service, Sanders County, and DNRC have placed area closures around the fires as they are integral to public safety and safe fire operations. Although areas around the fires have been closed, the majority of lands in the area are still open for all types of activities.

Fire Update: Cooler and moister weather conditions have caused the fires to be less active with little growth through the weekend. Some edges of the fires were still active, but overall fire activity is limited to within the lines. These weather conditions are allowing firefighters to suppress the fire directly by treating burning fuels on the perimeter of the fire. Aerial water drops by helicopters were done on areas with more active fire.

Weather and Fire Behavior: Monday will be cloudy as a cold front system will bring better chances of a steady rain. Wetting rain and cold temperatures will further limit fire activity.

Fire Details:

Sheep Gap – Crews continued constructing a line above Mountain Rd residences and to the east near the fire’s edge. They will continue this operation on Monday to protect the homes below and contain spread to the east. On the northwest flank above Eddy Creek the line was improved and will be held using water drops if needed. On the south side of the fire, crews will scout for opportunities for line construction and will patrol and improve existing line. The fire remained active late in the evening and crews remained alert to keep the fire out of the structures.

Deep Creek/Miller Creek – Helicopters dropped water on the fire around Benson Peak to slow the fire’s growth as it backed downslope. Crews held existing lines to keep the fire west of Graves Creek, and east of Deep Creek. Crews will work along the southwest face of the fire and go direct where possible to bring the fire down to an old road system above the homes. Operations will continue to hold the fire between Graves and Deep Creeks. The northeast flank will be scouted to identify possible locations for line construction. There was a slop over found across Graves Creek by crews and engines that has been contained.

Cub Creek – Crews finished removing snags along a control line above Cub Creek. Lines were constructed along existing roads above Haines Gulch. They will continue this work today to prevent further fire spread down Little Beaver Creek. Firefighters will establish control line, hold previously created black line, and patrol the roads around the southwest side of the fire.

Reader Fires – There was no change in activity on these fires. An engine crew will continue to monitor these fires.
