FACT SHEET Meyers Fire

September 5, 2017

Fire Information Line: (406) 859-9107 Office hours: 8:00 a.m. to 8:00 p.m.

Email: meyerswhetstone@gmail.com

Public Meeting Tonight in Philipsburg at 5:00 pm at Granite High School Cafeteria

Incident: The Meyers Fire was started by lightning on July 14, 2017. It merged with the Whetstone Fire (3,621 acre), also started by lightning, on July 13, 2017.

Location: 25 miles southwest of Philipsburg, Montana.

Agency Jurisdiction: USDA Forest Service, Beaverhead-Deerlodge and Bitterroot National Forests

Incident Management: Irv Leach’s Northern Rockies Type 2 Incident Management Team.

Current Size: 54,814 acres Resources Assigned: 344

Status: The fire is burning in mixed conifer stands, primarily lodgepole pine, Douglas fir, and subalpine fir, with numerous standing dead trees. There are residences still at risk in the Moose Lake, Frog Pond, Springer Memorial Park and Bonanza Lands areas.

Yesterday’s Activities: Overall fire activity moderated due to lower temperatures, smoky skies shading the fire, and a reduction in wind speed. Smoke limited helicopter use, but they were able to support water bucket drops southwest of Kaiser Lake up the Senate Creek drainage. Pumps and sprinklers are in working order around the residences. Frog Pond and Moose Lake structures have not been damaged by the fire growth around them. At the Springer Memorial and Bonanza Lands area, the hand line and machine line under constructions were prepped and brought in closer to the existing subdivisions. The containment dropped from five percent to zero based on the increases fire perimeter on Sunday.

Current Resources: Three 20-person crews, twenty engines, four pieces of heavy equipment (skidgines, dozers, masticators), ten water tenders, and three helicopters. Total personnel: 344.

Today’s Activities: Today will generally be warmer across the Meyers Fire area with light winds coming from the east. Fire behavior will be less affected by wind and driven more by terrain and should be similar to yesterday due to the smoke cover preventing the sun’s warmth heating the fuels. Structure protection measures are strong in resident areas of Frog Pond, Moose Lake, and East Fork Bitterroot. Some crews will be moving to the Bitterroot National Forest side of the fire to help work on the in-direct line around Spinger Memorial. Burning operations around Zeke’s Meadow and Moose Lake plan to bring fire closer to the lines built over the last few weeks.

Evacuations: The Frog Pond and Moose Lake areas are under an evacuation order. Additionally, an evacuation warning is in effect on East Fork Rd from FS Rd 5778 to Little East Fork Rd (FS RD 724) on the Bitterroot National Forest. Those under an evacuation warning should be prepared to leave at a moment’s notice.

Restrictions/Closures: Closure orders are in place on both National Forests. Bitterroot National Forest emergency closure orders are in effect for upper areas of the East Fork Bitterroot River. Beaverhead-Deerlodge National Forest has an emergency closure order around the fire extending into the Anaconda Pintler Wilderness. A temporary flight restriction (TFR) is in effect surrounding the Meyers Fire area. Granite County is under Stage 2 fire restrictions for private lands in the county. Amended Stage 1 fire restrictions are in effect today which prohibits ALL campfires in southwestern Montana on public lands, including in fire rings and developed recreation areas. For fire restriction details, visit firerestrictions.us/mt.

For further information, follow the Beaverhead-Deerlodge National Forest Facebook and Bitterroot National Forest Facebook pages.
