Meyers Sapphire Complex Update for 9-15-17

Fire Information Line: 406-859-9107 Email: meyerswhetstone@gmail.com

Inciweb: https://inciweb.nwcg.gov/incident/5364/ https://inciweb.nwcg.gov/incident/5397/

Facebook: Lolo National Forest Beaverhead-Deerlodge National Forest Bitterroot National Forest

Changes made to the forest closures please see “Closures” section below.

Due to wet, cold weather conditions this weekend, fireline staffing will change. Some firefighters will remain in patrol status, monitoring areas that continue to show fire activity. When conditions allow, rehabilitation work and mop up will continue. The health and welfare of our firefighters is a priority.

Sapphire Complex: 43,732 acres Location: Approximately 25 miles south-southeast of Missoula Containment: 60% Cause: Lightning Total Personnel: 179 Three fires comprise the Sapphire Complex: Little Hogback, Goat Creek, and Sliderock. The fires are in steep, rocky terrain between Rock Creek and Upper Willow Creek drainages. Little Hogback Fire: 34,552 acres On the east side of the fire, rehabilitation and patrol will remain in place as weather permits. Crews will be working on rehabilitation and moving equipment. Monitoring will continue and action taken as needed. Structure protection along Rock Creek Road from Wild Rose to Stony Creek will be removed as structures aren’t imminently threatened. In Idle Ranch Court and Trout Haven subdivision, structures are still prepped for protection. Goat Creek Fire: 8,300 acres: Firefighters completed 97 percent of the rehabilitation of constructed fire breaks. Most of the equipment has remained to help with rehabilitation on the Little Hogsback Fire. Firefighters will work on rehabilitation as weather permits. Sliderock Fire: 900 acres This fire is in patrol status; however, some heat remains in the interior. Rehabilitation of constructed firelines will continue as weather permits. Meyers Fire: 62,034 acres Location: Approximately 25 miles southwest of Philipsburg, Montana Containment: 8% Cause: Lightning Total Personnel: 311

The fire is burning in mixed conifer stands, primarily lodgepole pine, Douglas-fir, and subalpine fir, with many standing dead trees. These snags have become increasingly dangerous to firefighters as they burn through and get pushed over by wind.

Most of the firefighting equipment has been moved has been moved out of the Frog Pond area.

Currently, the Carpp Creek drainage at the eastern part of the fire remains a priority and will be monitored to keep the fire on the south side of the creek. Structure protection measures ahead of this drainage continue to be monitored for readiness and contingency fire breaks between Carpp Creek and Georgetown Lake are in place. The area around Zeke’s Meadows has had little fire activity and is being monitored.

Mechanical work on the fireline has been completed around the Billie Springer Memorial and Bonanza Lands subdivisions. Crews will continue cleaning up along this fireline. On the east side of the fire where it has crossed the Continental Divide into the Wise River Ranger District in Beaverhead and Deer Lodge counties. The fire will be monitored.

Resources Threatened

Sapphire Complex: Private property and structures in the Rock Creek and Upper Willow Creek drainages, as well as fisheries in Rock Creek, working ranches, commercial structures and businesses, and the Bonneville Power Administration powerline. Meyers Fire: Private property and structures in the Moose Lake, Frog Pond, Billie Springer Memorial Park, Bonanza Lands, Senate Mountain, Ross Fork, East Fork and Georgetown Lake areas, as well as other scattered dwellings and grazing lands, will continue to be monitored. Evacuations Sapphire Complex: There are no evacuations in place.

Meyers Fire: The Moose Lake and Frog area mandatory evacuation has been lifted. The Billie Springer Memorial, and Bonanza subdivisions are under warning for evacuation. Additionally, an evacuation warning is in effect on East Fork Road from FS Road 5778 to Little East Fork Rd (FS Rd 724) on the Bitterroot National Forest. Those under an evacuation warning should be prepared to leave at a moment’s notice.

Closures Closures are in place to protect the public from potential harm from wildfires and firefighting operations.

Sapphire Complex: The Rock Creek Road from I-90 south to Bitterroot Campground (MP 23.5) is OPEN. Due to fire traffic congestion and fire safety concerns, Rock Creek Road will stay CLOSED from Bitterroot Campground, south to Cornish Gulch (FSR5027), with limited access for private property owners only. The Beaverhead-Deerlodge National Forest reduced closure restrictions in the Black Pine ridge area, but maintains all area closures in Upper Willow Creek, North Fork Lower Willow Creek, and Harvey Creek areas.

Beaverhead-Deerlodge National Forest has issued order #2017-D8-039, rescinding order #2017-D8-027 (closure of North Fork Lower Willow Creek and Harvey Creek areas). The new order includes lands that are west of Upper Willow Creek, north of Scotchman Gulch, and south of Mountain Spring. Lands east of Upper Willow Creek in the Long John Creek, Rattling Gulch, Tipperary Creek, and Cordurory Creek drainages are also included.

Upper Willow Creek Road remains closed to the public with limited access for private property owners only. The Lolo National Forest still has area closures east and north of Rock Creek Road, south of I-90 and east to the Beaverhead-Deerlodge National Forest boundary.

The Bureau of Land Management has an area closure in Upper Willow Creek, Ram Mountain, and nearby areas. The Lolo and Beaverhead-Deerlodge National Forests, the Bureau of Land Management, and the Montana Department of Natural Resources (DNRC) closure orders and maps are posted under the closures tab on Inciweb. A temporary flight restriction (TFR) remains in effect surrounding the Little Hogback Fire area. Today’s map reflects the current closure areas.

Meyers Fire: Bitterroot National Forest emergency closure orders are still in effect for upper areas of the East Fork Bitterroot River. The expanded Beaverhead-Deerlodge National Forest closure also remains in effect. On the south side of the fire, the closure extends between Bender Creek and LaMarche Creek, including Pintler Campground and Trailhead, Mystic Trailhead, Mussigbrod Campground and Trailhead, and all trailheads in the Mudd Creek and Fishtrap Creek areas. Several miles of the Continental Divide Trail are closed. A TFR is still in place surrounding the Meyers Fire area. Granite County is under Stage 2 fire restrictions for private lands in the county. Amended Stage 1 fire restrictions are in effect prohibiting ALL campfires in southwestern Montana on public lands, including in fire rings and developed recreation areas. For fire restriction details, visit firerestrictions.us/mt.

Weather

An area of low pressure dropped out of Canada yesterday with precipitation throughout today and tonight. Rain and snow will continue through today. The snow level will drop down to 5,500 feet on Friday. Today’s high temperatures will be low 30’s to the mid 20’s.

For further information, visit https://inciweb.nwcg.gov and select “Meyers Fire” or “Sapphire Complex” in Montana, and follow the Beaverhead-Deerlodge National Forest Facebook, Bitterroot National Forest Facebook or Lolo National Forest pages.
