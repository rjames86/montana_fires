Daily update for 9/9

Incident: Crucifixion Creek Fire Wildfire
Released: 32 min. ago

Wildfire Information

Crucifixion Creek, Strawberry Creek, & Scalp Fires

Rocky Mountain Ranger District

Saturday, September 9, 2017

Contact: Rocky Mountain Ranger District, 406-466-5341

Media Contact: Duane Buchi, (406) 750-8934

Location: CRUCIFIXION CREEK Approximately 10 miles southwest of Heart Butte, in the Badger-Two Medicine area; STRAWBERRY CREEK—Approximately 5 miles southwest of Swift Dam, outside of Dupuyer; SCALP—Approximately 7 miles due west of Teton Pass Ski Area.

Fire Management & Activity: These fires are being managed with a confine and contain strategy due to their remote location, fire/fuel break from recent fire events, and limited availability of ground/aerial resources resulting from a large number of fires throughout the western States. A long-term management plan is in effect for coordination of management actions for each fire’s likely long duration. A Type 3 Incident Management Team took responsibility for the Crucifixion Creek, Strawberry Creek, & Scalp fires yesterday evening. The team is based out of Choteau, MT. Two helicopters and two dozers are assigned to these fires. The dozers will be staged to support contingencies as needed. All acreages are updated from overnight (IR) flight mapping. Due to poor visibility from smoke inversions, ground personnel were unable to view much for fire behavior.

Crucifixion Creek Fire Update (8,191 Acres): Fire personnel started mop up activity around Badger Cabin yesterday and will continue securing the area today. Weather and visibility permitting, aircraft will resupply and swap out the crew from Badger Cabin. The northern edge of the fire is still between the confluence of Red Poacher and Dry Creeks, and west to Lee Creek. Yesterday’s heavy smoke kept aviation resources grounded, but crews are prepared to reengage today with helicopter water drops in the north and northeast flank of the fire to slow it from moving east.

Strawberry Creek Fire Update (1,340 Acres): Ground crews were able to confirm the fire has moved to Strawberry Creek Trail #161 and East Fork Strawberry Creek Trail #371. The northeast finger of the fire is moving slowly toward the continental divide and this will be a focus for aviation to check with water drops as weather permits. Structure protection at Sabido Cabin is complete.

Scalp Fire Update (11,425 Acres): Fire personnel reported the fire continues to creep through portions of the 2007 Fool Creek fire area. 5 personnel are staged at the Gates Cabin, traveling daily to Wrong Creek Cabin to conduct fuel mitigation.

Heart Butte Air Quality: As of 9:00am today, the Montana DEQ forecasted Heart Butte as “ Unhealthy.” There is an online reference at http://deq.mt.gov/Air/FireUpdates/VisibilityRanges to help people make informed decisions on outdoor activities relative to forecasted air quality. These are also posted at the Heart Butte Post Office.

Weather: RED FLAG WARNING in effect from noon today to midnight tonight. FIRE WEATHER WATCH in effect from Sunday afternoon through Sunday evening. Widespread critical fire weather conditions are expected through this evening, with elevated to critical conditions possible on Sunday. The main concerns today will be increasing winds/low RH and isolated dry thunderstorms with gusty outflow winds. The main concern on Sunday is gusty winds and low RH. Elevated fire conditions may continue into early next week.

Public Meeting: Wednesday, September 13, 2017 at 6 p.m. at the Heart Butte School-Multi-Purpose Room

Additional Information: Due to recent concerns regarding the Strawberry Creek Fire, the Pondera County Commissioners closed Swift Dam Campground west of Dupuyer. An area closure is in effect for public safety on the Flathead and Helena-Lewis and Clark National Forests. See map on reverse for illustration of trail closures.
