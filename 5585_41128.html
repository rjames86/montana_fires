West Fork and Moose Peak Fires Daily Fact Sheet 9.15.17

Incident: West Fork Fire Wildfire
Released: 1 day ago

Fire Information Number: (406) 209-9961 Evacuation Information: (406) 293-6295

Location: West Fork Fire is 7 miles northwest of Libby, MT Size: 16,940

Cause: Lightning Containment: 19% Personnel: 262

Email: WestForkMoosePeakFires@gmail.com InciWeb: https://inciweb.nwcg.gov/incident/5592/

Location: Moose Peak Fire originated near Moose Peak in Sanders County, MT Size: 11,587

Cause: Lightning Containment: 0% Personnel: 144

Email: WestForkMoosePeakFires@gmail.com InciWeb: https://inciweb.nwcg.gov/incident/5583/

Visit Lincoln County Webpage www.lincolncountymt.us to sign-up for the County Code Red system.

Weather: Temperatures are expected to be in the upper 50s with mostly clear skies after the morning inversion lifts. Winds will be light and from the east. Cooler weather is expected to moderate fire activity again today with slightly warmer and drier conditions expected for the weekend. Rain is in the forecast for early next week.

West Fork Fire Yesterday: Firefighters increased containment to 19% on the lower Quartz Creek and Bobtail Creek Roads. Helicopters made drops on the spot fire near Gold Hill. Hotshot crews completed burnout on the north flank of the fire to secure firelines. Crews worked to improve dozer lines on the south and west sides of the fire near Quartz Creek Rd. Engine crews patrolled to monitor fire conditions overnight.

Today’s Planned Actions: Crews will be working to build and improve fireline on the north flank of the fire, and will continue to monitor the spot fire near Gold Hill. A potential aerial ignition is being considered east of Mt Tom to the Pipe Creek Road. Air operations will continue as conditions allow to support suppression efforts. Structure assessment and protection will continue throughout the fire area. Crews will continue to use mechanized fuel break construction along Pipe Creek Road area to improve fire line perimeter.

West Fork Fire Evacuations: Some evacuation orders have been lifted. Please check the Lincoln County Sheriff’s Facebook page or call (406) 293-6295 for the most up-to-date information on evacuations.

West Fork Fire Closures: The Kootenai National Forest has issued a closure order in effect for the West Fork Fire. The closure order and map are located under the closure tab on the Inciweb Page.

Moose Peak Yesterday: Despite cooler temperatures and higher humidity the fire continued to be moderately active in the Fisher Creek area to the north and Fishtrap Creek on the east. Hotshot crews continued to improve firelines and Super Scooper aircraft made drops in these areas to support fire suppression. Firefighters continued to make structure assessments and are implementing protection measures. The Deep Creek Fire located a few miles to the south east of the Moose Peak Fire is being monitored by aerial resources.

Today’s Planned Actions: Firefighters will continue to construct dozer lines and mechanized fuel breaks northwest of Miller Lake in the East Fisher Creek area and on the east flank in the Fishtrap Creek area. Structure assessment and protection will continue throughout the fire area. Air operations will continue as conditions allow supporting suppression efforts.

Moose Peak Fire Pre - Evacuations: Pre-Evacuation notices have been issued for East Fisher Road, Bayhorse Ranch and McGinnis Meadows. Please check the Lincoln County Sheriff’s Facebook page or call (406) 293-6295 for the most up-to-date information on evacuations.

Moose Peak Fire Closures: Sylvan Lake campground has been closed. The Kootenai National Forest has issued a closure order in effect for the Moose Peak Fire. The closure order and map are located under the closure tab on the Inciweb Page.
