Incident Overview

The Sunrise and Burdette Fires are being managed by the South West Montana All Hazard Incident Management Team under the leadership of Incident Commander Ken Parks. Both the Sunrise and Burdette Fires are 95% contained.

Brush

Fuel loading is variable and is dependent upon the aspect across the landscape. Primarily a surface fire burning in downed fuels. Fire scars to the west of the current fire perimeter and provide moderated fire behavior.

Significant Events

The South West corner of the fire was active today with short uphill runs and group torching. Fire personnel assisted the Lolo N.F. with two Initial Attacks providing 1 Type 3 helicopter, 2 Single Engine Air Tankers (SEATs) and air attack.

Outlook

Planned Actions

Air Curtain Burner operation ceased indefinitely.

North (DIV H/J)

Suppression rehab is 95% completed. Rehab will begin again on 9-13-17 East (DIV K,L,M) Unstaffed

South (DIV-T)

Continued efforts to keep fire in check with air resources.

-Continue to refine pumping operations in Quartz creek. Continue to scout, prep ,treat and hold containment lines north and west of structure 10.

-Continue to monitor fire growth and prepare the occupants of Quartz Creek for evacuation if fire crosses management action point for evacuation orders.

-Plumb containment line west of structure 10 for long term sprinkler use

-Utilize helicopter bucket work and SEATs to check the fires advance off the ridge between 46 Creek and Quartz Creek. -Recon the Quartz/46 Creek ridge for opportunities to use retardant as a check line

-Patrol Quartz creek Rd and mop up earlier firing operation

-Locate and Identify all pumps

West (DIV-C)

-Monitor fire spread and check progression as appropriate.

-Mop up where needed to secure lines

-Patrol the mid-slope Rd south of DP2 for rollout

Movement of slash to the Mineral County gravel pit should be completed by end of shift today.

Projected Incident Activity

Minimal fire activity on Burdette.

-North (DIV H/J)

Continued smoldering. Fire will continue to burn heavy downed fuels and stumps

-East (DIV K/L/M)

Minimal fire activity anticipated. Fire will continue to burn heavy interior fuels

-South (DIV T) with lower humidity and higher temps today fire became more active with group torching and uphill runs.

Given the steep terrain, numerous snags, and limited resources containment could be hampered.

West (DIV C) Uncontained line continues to grow making small runs outside current perimeter edging closer to Quartz Creek. Air resources used to keep fire in check. If fire establishes itself in Quartz creek, this will cross the MAP for evacuation orders in Quartz Creek. The residents in Quartz Creek and McFarland remain under an Evacuation Warning.

24 hours: Slightly cooler and slightly higher humidity predicted but with 25 mph gusts predicted could hamper fire control efforts.

48 hours: Rain Possible

72 hours: Rain Possible

Anticipated after 72 hours: Cooler Temps in the forecast

Remarks

An area closure remains in effect on the Lolo National Forest where fireline repairs are underway northwest of Trout Creek. West Trout Creek Road closure rescinded at 1200 hours Mountain Time 9/11/17.

TFR instated on Sunrise fire. The TFR is the current fire perimeter.

Hot dry weather today, slightly cooler and lower relative humidity tomorrow (9/13). Rain forecasted for the end of the week.
