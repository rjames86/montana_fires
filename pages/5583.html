Location: The Highway 200 Complex fires are located in Sanders County, MT near the towns of Thompson Falls and Plains. The Sheep Gap fire is approximately 2 miles west of Plains. The remaining complex fires are located 8 miles north and west of Thompson Falls.

Evacuations and Closures: The Sanders County Sheriff's Office has lifted the evacuation notice. The previous evacuated areas of Mountain Road and Swamp Creek now have pre-evacuation warnings in place. Members of the Montana Army National Guard are assisting with road closures, pre-evacuations, and any other assistance that may be needed. The Forest Service, Sanders County, and DNRC have placed area closures around the fires as they are integral to public safety and safe fire operations. Although areas around the fires have been closed, the majority of lands in the area are still open for all types of activities. Visit the official Highway 200 Complex Fire InciWeb page for details and maps about closure areas.

Fire Update: Cooler and moister weather conditions have caused the fires to be less active with little growth. Some edges of the fires were still active, but overall fire activity is limited to within the lines. These weather conditions are allowing firefighters to suppress the fire more directly by treating burning fuels on the perimeter of the fire. As suppression objectives are met, fireline rehabilitation needs will be assessed.

Weather and Fire Behavior: Tuesday will be cloudy as the cold front system moves through the area bringing chances of showers. Wetting rain and cold temperatures will further limit fire activity.

Fire Details:

Sheep Gap – Crews and a timber task force are constructing a line above Mountain Rd residences and to the east along the fire’s edge. They will continue this operation on Tuesday to protect the homes below and contain spread to the east. On the northwest flank above Eddy Creek the crews will do a heavy mop up to reduce the amount of accumulated heat. On the south side of the fire, crews will scout for opportunities for line construction and will patrol and improve existing line.

Deep Creek/Miller Creek – Fire resources will continue improving the fire line and use more direct tactics to keep the fire west of Graves Creek, and east of Deep Creek. Fire personnel will work along the southwest face of the fire and go direct where possible to bring the fire down to an old road system above the homes. The northeast flank will be scouted to identify possible locations and opportunities for line construction. The slop over found across Graves Creek was contained by crews and engines that will continue mop up efforts and hold the line.

Cub Creek- Remaining hot spots on the south side will be pulled apart to allow moisture and cold temperatures to extinguish fuels. On the north end firefighters will reduce hazard trees as they hold and monitor along the existing road systems. The structure group has completed structure assessment and will continue to monitor and protect the houses.

Reader Fires –The fires remained docile. They will continue to be monitored and staffed as needed.

Current Weather

Weather Concerns

"Rain moved across the Complex area on Sunday night and Monday. Rainfall totals reached the quarter to one third of an inch range in Branch II (Sheep Gap) and the 0.4 to 0.5 inch range in Branch I. Cloud cover persisted with below normal temperatures and higher humilities observed today.

An upper level trough will slowly move across the Western U.S. and into the Northern Rockies through mid to late week. This will keep the weather unsettled through Thursday with periodic chances for precipitation, well below normal temperatures, gusty winds at times on the ridges, and persistent clouds. Another system moving through the trough will bring a chance of widespread precipitation with a wetting rain at midweek.

This trough will begin to depart by Thursday night, with chances for showers diminishing and a period of warming and drying expected to follow by the weekend.
