Incident Overview

Update: Sept 13th

Location: Highway 200 corridor, near Plains and Thompson Falls, MT

Agency Jurisdiction: Lolo and Kootenai National Forests

Incident Command: Western Montana Type II Incident Management Team, Incident Commander - Rick Connell

Evacuation Update from Sanders County Sheriff's Office (10 PM, 09/12): In the Sheep Gap Fire area - Swamp Creek Road (from 4-corners to the Forest Service boundary) has been evacuated by Sanders County Sheriff's Office. At 9 PM, Sept 12th, the area will be closed by the Sheriff's department. Anyone found in this area, on property not their own, will be arrested for trespassing. Residents along Black Jack and Mountain Roads have been issued pre-evacuation warnings. Affected residents are being contacted individually by law enforcement officials.

Special Message: Fire management can be extremely dynamic. For updates, please rely on official sources including the Facebook and InciWeb sites listed at the top of this page. To confirm information, you can also call the fire info line at (406) 219-1960. And remember, when acting on changes to information, do so in a calm, rational, and respectful way.

Overview

Current Status: Yesterday afternoon brought increased winds and more fire activity. As the critical fire weather moved through the area, all fires within the Highway 200 Complex experienced increased fire behavior. In several areas, spot fires that ignited in thick timber and fuels produced large smoke columns. Despite this increase if fire activity, previous containment lines are still intact and fire lines near structures continue to hold. As of 6 AM, no structures have been lost.

Outlook: Wednesday will likely see increasing clouds with a chance of an isolated shower or thunderstorm. This potential weather change would help to moderate fire behavior. However, the risk of fire spread still exists from outflow winds created as the storm system moves past. A confine and contain strategy is being implemented due to steep, inaccessible terrain and heavy fuel loading with point protection for values at risk. Firefighters will continue to focus on structure protection and holding existing containment line. Firefighter and public safety remain the top priority.

Closures: Area closures are in place around the immediate fire areas and are integral to safe fire operations. As hunting season continues, please respect area closures. The Forest Service is working to ensure that closures are kept to a minimum but are large enough for firefighter and public safety. Please see InciWeb and the Forest Service webpages for the latest closure area maps.

Total Size: 24,225 acres

Personnel: 308

Infrared Flight:

Infrared flights are often used at night to get an accurate account of the fire perimeter by mapping heat sources. However, the infrared flights were unable to be flown last night. We recognize that fire size likely increased during yesterday’s fire activity and we will be reported acreage as information comes in, directly from firefighters on the fire line.

Fire Details:

Sheep Gap – 40% Contained – 13,414 acres: Last night, the Sanders County Sheriff’s Office issued evacuations for residents along Swamp Creek Road from 4-corners to the Forest Service boundary. Pre-Evacuation orders were also extended for the Black Jack and Mountain Roads. Today, firefighters will continue to provide structure protection along the east flank of the fire. They will also patrol for, and suppress, any spot fires that resulted from last night’s increased fire activity.

Deep Creek – 0% Contained - 5,733 acres: A Pre-Evacuation order is still in effect East of the Clark Fork River in the Blue Slide Area. The fire is continuing to back downhill towards Graves Creek as well as working its way across slopes. Firefighters continue to hold the fire at existing mechanical line and hand line. Hose lay is also being utilized to keep fire severity to a minimum inside fire lines and to suppress spot fires outside the fire lines. Smoke will be visible as areas within the fire continue to burn.

Cub Creek – 0% Contained- 4,034 acres: Pre-Evacuation orders are in effect for an area west of Highway 200 from White Pine Creek to Cherry Creek. The south finger of the continues to be minimally active. The north and east sections, however, continue to threaten holding lines and challenge firefighters. Ground crews are being assisted by aerial resources to hold the fire in check and suppress spot fires beyond fire lines.

Reader Fires – 95% Contained - 152 acres (total): The Reader 1 and Reader 2 fires continue to hold at existing fire lines. Mop-up activities continue and resources are being redeployed to the Cub Creek Fire as they become available.

Miller Creek – 0% Contained - 892 acres: The fire made a significant run to the east yesterday. Firefighters continue to monitor and patrol the fire for unwanted fire growth but this fire remains lower on the priority list due to its distance from values at risk. Resources are in the area and will respond to take appropriate suppression actions as needed.

Outlook

Planned Actions

Sheep Gap:

-Look for opportunities to check the fire spread west of DP 106 with the goal of tying the fire into natural rock barrier and foot trail.

-Continue to slowly bring fire from subdivision at H200 west along the dozerline until it ties into Poachers Gulch.

-Provide structure protection north and east side of the fire along River Rd West.

Deep Creek:

-The fire has reached Graves Creek and is flanking across slope, both up and down drainage. Patrol and pretreat fuels between Graves Creek and Graves Creek Rd.

-Patrol Deep Creek for rollout and spotting where the fire has advanced or is backing downslope towards the creek.

-Use in-place hose lay and sprinklers to protect wooden upper bridge if necessary.

-Utilize helicopter assets on assets on the southwest portion of fire to prevent it form hooking around the ridge.

Cub Creek:

-Patrol the 2262 road and west of the 301 road for spotting and rollout where the fire has advanced to it.

-Continue line construction and mastication using heavy equipment and crews on the 301 road.

-Continue burnout to the east from DP46 to DP47

-Use air resources to keep north finger and Hines Gulch in check.

Reader:

Patrol and mopup as needed.

Miller: Miller is being monitored for fire growth. The goal is to keep the fire South of the 2207 Rd and west of the 367 rd located in Miller Creek and Vermillion drainages.

-Monitor and patrol the Upper Vermilion Rd for spotting and rollout. Hold the fire to the west of the upper Vermilion Rd.

-Monitor for spot fires across upper Vermilion Rd. If needed, use air resources to check spread dependent on higher priority missions within the Highway 200 Complex.

Projected Incident Activity

Wednesday 9-13:

-Sheep Gap, Deep Creek, Cub Creek, Miller Peak: Hot and The threshold for moderate fire behavior with torching, spotting, and possible uphill runs is 10 mph winds. Isolated thunderstorm potential with risk of outflow winds. Rollout is a significant safety concern.

-Reader and Reader 2: The fire should hold containment lines.

Remarks

The overall containment for the complex is 17%. But please note that as individual fire size changes in relation to the overall acreage this percentage may fluctuate. Deep Creek Cub Creek, and Miller Creek remain at 0% containment. Reader 1 is 95% contained, Reader 2 is 95%, and Sheep Gap is 40% contained.

Current Weather

Weather Concerns

On Wednesday, there will be increasing clouds. Conditions will also be breezy with a chance of an isolated shower or thunderstorm. Maximum temperatures will be 76-80 on ridges, 81-85 mid-slopes, and 82-86 in the valley. Winds will be from the west/southwest at 8-14 mph, gusting to 18 in the afternoon.
