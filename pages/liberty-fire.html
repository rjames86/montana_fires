Significant Events

Narrative:

Temperatures still linger in the lower 80's and relative humidities remain in mid to upper teens. Skies

were mostly clear allowing fire activity to increase earlier in the day. As fire becomes active group

torching occurs in the Gold Creek Peak and Black Mountain areas, while fire continues to back into

Liberty Meadows. The fire remains active in the Gold Creek Meadows and South Fork Jocko River area

and continues its westward advancement into the Rattlesnake Wilderness area and the Flathead South

Fork Jocko Tribal Primitive Area.

Outlook

Planned Actions

Resources on Divisions M, T, and U continue to maintain, improve, patrol, and hold containment lines,suppress spot fires, and mitigate hazard trees near road systems, through the use of mechanized

equipment and handcrews, to impede fires progress. As weather conditions allow, firing operations off

containnment lines to deepen said lines will continue. Aircraft is being used when available to support

checking of fire growth and control spots. Divisions A, B, and C continue to be in monitor status in areas

where fire continues to burn in the Rattlesnake Wilderness and the South Jocko Tribal Primitive Area.

Some checking action has been underway in the western part of Division C. Suppression repair has

begun on Divisions A and B and suppression repair needs are being evaluated on Division C and M. Fire resources continue to provide initial attack capability of new starts within the TFR.

Projected Incident Activity

12 hours:

Hot and dry weather conditions remained over fire today as temperatures rose to the upper

70's to 80's and relative humidity dropped into the upper teens. Skies were mostly clear throughout the day, and winds were somewhat gusty. Fire was active mostly on Divisions A, B, C, and U, where little suppression work has been accomplished to date. Fire is mostly

moving uphill in Divisions A and B, through heavy dead and downed fuels, with some torching. Fire is backing slowly downhill toward Liberty Creek, and should reach the riparian area by sometime Saturday, September 2. Fire on Divisions M and T continues to burn within primary

containment lines. The potential of spotting over containment lines is greatly reduced, as the line is blackened and effective mop-up is accomplished.

24 hours:

Gusty west to northwest winds should persist over the fire area, under mostly sunny skies.

Temperatures should increase into the 80's, relative humidity should be in the low- to mid-teens, with Haines Index of 4. Fire will remain active on Divisions A, B, C and U with uphill crown runs possible, and backing continuing into Liberty Creek in Division C. Heat in divisions M and T will continue to decrease, as large fuels burn out and are mopped up. Potential for spotting over containment lines is rapidly decreasing.

48 hours:

High pressure continues to build, and winds will be mostly terrain driven. Fire should reach

some points of the riparian area adjacent to Liberty Creek today. Fire will remain active in the

Primitive Area of Divisions A, B and U. Decreasing fire activity is expected in Divisions M, T, and the lined portion of U.

72 hours:

High pressure remains with mostly sunny conditions, temperatures warming and humidity decreasing to the mid-teens. Weather conditions continue to support active fire within the

Primitive Area of Divisions A and B. Backing will continue in Division C toward Liberty Creek.

Anticipated after 72 hours:

High pressure with warmer and drier conditions and decreased winds will

support moderate to active fire within Divisions A and B. Burnout of large fuels

should be nearly complete in Divisions M, T, and U.

Remarks

Confine Contain strategy has not changed. Reduction in percent completion from original several weeks

is due to a more accurate reflection of remaining operational needs and reporting strategy.

The operational strategy is due to factors including elevated risk for personnel constructing direct line in

heavy timber and snags with a low probability of success. Instead an indirect containment strategy is

used with natural fuel barriers and light fuels with little or no hazardous snags and constructed fire line.

Approximately 33.7 miles of indirect line has been constructed and 12.9 miles of direct containment line

has been completed. Indirect containment lines are not complete or considered "controlled" lines until

the fire has reached those lines or they have been burnt out. Control means little likelihood that fire will

escape the line. Change in completion percent reflects that the east side of the fire will not be

completed until fire is held at the lines - approximately 70% of current fire perimeter. The remaining

30%, or west side of fire, will be managed under a confinement strategy within Wilderness and

Primitive areas and few containment lines will actually be put in place due to resource concerns, low

probability of spread, and increased risk in heavy timber.

Management Action Points have been developed for implementation in the long term strategy.

Forecasted:

Dry conditions will continue on Saturday as the upper level high pressure continues to build over the region. Minimum humidity

recovery is expected to be poor, only recovering into the 30th percentile. Highs on Saturday are expected to climb into the lower

to mid-80s in the higher elevations and into the lower 90s in the

valleys. Humidity will drop into the teens across the entire incident.

Winds are forecast to increase to the WSW at 10 to 15 mph during the afternoon with gusts near 20 mph along the ridge tops.
