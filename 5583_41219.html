Highway 200 Complex Daily Update, September 16, 8 a.m.

Incident: Highway 200 Complex Wildfire
Released: 3 hrs. ago

Approximate Size (acres/%containment):
Sheep Gap –24,816 acres/40%
Deep & Miller Creek –15,894 acres/0%

Cub Creek –5,690 acres/11%
Readers – 152 acres/95%
TOTAL – 46,552 acres/10%

Total personnel: 336

Location: The Highway 200 Complex fires are located in Sanders County, MT near the towns of Thompson Falls and Plains. The Sheep Gap fire is approximately 2 miles west of Plains. The remaining complex fires are located 8 miles north and west of Thompson Falls.

Evacuations and Closures: The Sanders County Sheriff's Office has evacuated Swamp Creek Road from Four Corners to the intersection of the East and West fork of Swamp Creek. Pre-evacuations were completed on Blackjack Rd. from Four Corners to where it intersects River Rd West. Additional areas under evacuation orders include Glades Ranch, Cat Track Lane to Mountain subdivision and the residences between that are south of Blackjack Road, and Foothill Lane. These areas have been closed by the Sheriff’s office. Members of the Montana Army National Guard are assisting with road closures, pre-evacuations, evacuations, and any other assistance that may be needed. The Forest Service has also placed area closure orders around the fires as they are integral to public safety and safe fire operations. Although areas around the fires have been closed, the majority of lands in the area are still open for all types of activities.

Fire Update: Fire activity was limited Friday with the cooler, moister conditions. There was no significant fire growth and activity was mainly limited to spread on the ground with a small amount of single tree torching. The fires were observed and mapped from the air to provide better information on the perimeter. Acres reported reflect this mapping effort. Previous acres were based on infrared flights that were obscured by cloud cover. The increase in acres is a result of this improved mapping, not because of fire growth today. Helicopters dropped about 120 buckets of water on the fires to calm fire spread. More crews and equipment are arriving to the fire as other fires release these resources because of improving conditions. Even with a quieter fire and improved weather conditions, Incident Commander Irv Leach told the crews at briefing “We aren’t letting our guard down. There is still plenty of work to do to protect the high values at risk. We’re in this for the long haul.”

Weather and Fire Behavior: Part of Sheep Gap received rain and snow at the highest elevation; the remainder and the other fires were dry. The recent moist system will leave the area and be replaced by a high pressure system. Temperatures and humidity will rebound with slight warming and drying through the weekend. Winds will be light from the west. Another storm system is expected to arrive on Monday with another chance for precipitation. Fire behavior will increase Saturday as fuels warm and dry, but it is not expected to be as active as earlier in the week. The northeast part of Deep-Miller fire near Lone Tree Peak may see the greatest potential for active fire growth as it moves downslope.

Fire Details:

Sheep Gap – A dozer line from the existing line on the northeast flank was constructed above residences. Opportunities for additional line were scouted in advance of this line. On the northwest flank above Eddy Creek the line was improved above the houses. Line construction on the southeast side will continue Saturday with the plan to extend around the eastern flank and scout around the fire for additional line opportunities. Line on the northwest flank will be held and improved.

Deep Creek/Miller Creek – Helicopters dropped water on the fire above houses to slow the fire’s growth as it backed downslope. There had been considerable fire growth prior to the arrival of the weather system on the northeast flank. Much of this was spotty with unburned fuels scattered in the perimeter. This area was scouted in advance for placement of possible line construction. On Saturday crews will hold existing lines and fire movement will be slowed as it continues to back downslope.

Cub Creek – Line construction occurred above Little Beaver Creek on the fire’s south side. On the north side, the fire was kept in check with helicopter water drops above Haines Gulch. Saturday crews will continue to hold existing lines and minimize fire movement with helicopters.

Reader Fires – There was no change in activity on these fires.
