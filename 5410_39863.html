SEPTEMBER 3, 2017

AUGUSTA, MT –Firefighters saw the Alice Creek Fire grow an additional 4,153 acres in the last 24 hours, bringing the total acres to 11,593. This growth was primarily west towards the Scapegoat Wilderness; north and east towards Surgarloaf Mountain; east along Bedrock Creek towards highway 200 and south into Wildcat Gulch. The fire did NOT move southeast towards the Elk Meadows community.

Due to a growing threat from the Alice Creek Fire, Lewis and Clark County Sheriff issued additional mandatory evacuations orders early this morning for residents on both sides of Highway 200, south of Highway 434, from mile marker 92 through mile marker 100, extending east to the South Fork of the Dearborn River. For safety, law enforcement requests residents to vacate their property as soon as practical.

Road Closures: Highway 200 remains closed between 279 and 434. County Sheriff Officers are controlling traffic along the Highway and will open it as soon as it is safe to do so. The Highway will remain closed until at least 1 p.m. today.

Fire engines are scattered along the Roadways off Highway 200 extinguishing hot spots and assessing the area to ensure there were no structures destroyed during the night. Dozers are also establishing containment lines around the eastern flank which will expedite highway 200 opening.

Linemen from the NorthWestern Energy power company are assessing the powerline that crosses Highway 200 and associated infrastructure today. The powerline is still charged.

A community meeting to receive an update on the fire situation will take place at 6 p.m. Monday evening at the Augusta School (410 Broadway, Augusta, MT).

The Dearborn Community has not received a pre-evacuation notice at this time. While the fire is moving in a northerly direction east of Blowout Mountain, fire leadership anticipates a northwesterly wind will blow the fire east, towards Little Skunk Creek today.

Pre-evacuation notices were given in the Alice Creek Basin and Tom's Gulch area last night. The Fire became very active in Wildcat Gulch (section 16 and 17), approximately 14 miles northeast of Lincoln, Montana. There is one active ranch and approximately 10 cabins affected by the pre-evacuation order.

There have not been any injuries or notifications of burned structures.

A Red Cross Shelter is available at the Wolf Creek Elementary School (150 Walsh St, Wolf Creek, MT).

A Red Flag Warning remains in effect through 9 p.m. Monday, September 4 for very dry and windy conditions. Incident meteorologists are forecasting erratic wind gusts near 30 mph, very much like conditions seen Saturday.

Forest managers are enlarging a forest area closure today due to extensive fire activity. This closure order will be posted at on the Forest’s Facebook Page, Forest Web Site and on InciWeb at: https://inciweb.nwcg.gov/incident/closures/5410/

To sign up to receive emergency notification messages from the Lewis and Clark County Sheriff, register for Smart911 at: https://www.smart911.com/smart911/registration/registrationLanding.action

For tips on how can prepare your home and family for an evacuation, visit: http://www.readyforwildfire.org/Pre-Evacuation-Preparation/
