FACT SHEET

DATE OF DETECTION: Saturday, July 22, 2017

CAUSE: Lightning

CURRENT ·ESTIMATED· SIZE: 21,393 acres

CONTAINMENT: 0%

  • As of 10p.m. tonight, residents only of Landers Fork, Elk Trail Park and Alice Creek may return to their homes. Residents are considered on pre-evacuation notice. There is heavy equipment working in these areas. Residents are asked to please drive carefully and slowly to as to not impede heavy equipment.

  • In Green Creek, single tree and group tree torching kept firefighters active throughout the day putting out hot spots created by the burning embers. As ranchers and other community members see activity, firefighters are answering questions and working with them to adapt to changes.

  • Firefighters are shifting heavy equipment south into Alice Creek where there is a greater need for them. Firefighters shift resources to meet the demands of the fire. This includes moving dozers, feller bunchers and skidgens into the area so they can begin removing dead standing trees, widening existing roads and removing debris.

  • Along Alice Creek Road, two new dozers will begin working with feller bunchers to widen the road, and begin constructing containment line. Aviators are also working with property owners to identify dip sites for aircraft that will reduce turnaround times to refill buckets.

  • Firefighters are assessing structures, road systems, and defensible space for structures located in the Alice Creek drainage.

  • Firefighters discovered a 50 acre spot fire by Alice Creek Ranch last night and spent the day constructing handline to contain it.

  • On the west side of Green Mountain, and east of Alice Creek Ranch, a feller buncher is beginning to widen the road, and mitigating snags firefighters have not been able to remove without heavy equipment.

  • On the northwest flank of the fire, west of the Continental Divide, the fire is now backing down the divide. Aircraft that have already been flying over the fire are monitoring this growth which is not posing an immediate threat to structures, natural or cultural resources.

  • Aircraft are a key asset in minimizing the fire’s growth. In the past 10 days, over 736,000 gallons of water have been dropped over the fire. Water takes the heat out of soils, increases humidity in the environment and lowers the surrounding temperatures. By changing the micro-climate of the timber, firefighters are given more time to construct containment line further from the fire’s edge where they can be more successful when the environment dries out again. In areas that are inaccessible, aircraft are effectively keeping the fire behavior more subdued which prevents additional spot fires from occurring.

  • Evacuation orders remain in effect for Tom’s Gulch area and the Elk Meadows/Evergreen Subdivision. Landers Fork, Elk Trail, Alice Creek Basin areas are on pre-evacuation notices. The Dearborn Canyon residents have NOT received a pre-evacuation notice; however, they should be ready to leave, should conditions rapidly change.

  • There have been four confirmed structures located at the end of Bedrock Road that have been lost. No other structures have been reported. The Lewis and Clark County Sheriff is an essential partner in helping firefighters with this work and will be the notifying owners if structures have been destroyed.

  • The Forest expanded the area closure as a result of the aggressive and widespread fire activity occurring across the Forest. A map of the Closure Order can be found at: https://inciweb.nwcg.gov/incident/article/5410/39087/

  • To sign up to receive emergency notification messages from the Lewis and Clark County Sheriff, register for Smart911 at: https://www.smart911.com/smart911/registration/registrationLanding.action
