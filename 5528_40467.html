Daily Update 9/8

Incident: Crucifixion Creek Fire Wildfire
Released: 13 min. ago

Wildfire Information

Crucifixion Creek Fire

Rocky Mountain Ranger District

Friday, September 8, 2017

Contact: Rocky Mountain Ranger District, 406-466-5341

Media Contact: Duane Buchi, (406)750-8934

Estimated Size: 8,191 acres

Location: Approximately 10 miles Southwest of Heart Butte, in the Badger-Two Medicine area

Fire Management & Activity: This fire is managed with a confine and contain strategy due to its remote location, fire/fuel break from recent fire events, and limited availability of ground/aerial resources resulting from a large number of fires in Montana and western States. A long-term management plan is in effect for coordination of management actions throughout the fire’s duration.

Fire Update: A Type 3 Incident Management Team will take responsibility for the Crucifixion Creek, Strawberry Creek, & Scalp fires at 8:00pm this evening. The team will be based in Choteau, MT. Two helicopters are assigned to these fires. The fire reached the Badger Cabin yesterday evening and burned through the surrounding area. Structure protection efforts were successful and fire personnel were able to go in behind the burn and begin mop up actions. The cabin survived the fire. The northern edge of the fire is now between the confluence of Red Poacher and Dry Creeks, and west to Lee Creek. Helicopters will continue to conduct water drops in the north and northeast flank of the fire to slow it from moving east.

Air Quality: As of 9:00am today, the Montana DEQ forecasted Heart Butte as “ Very Unhealthy.” There is an online reference at http://deq.mt.gov/Air/FireUpdates/VisibilityRanges to help people make informed decisions on outdoor activities relative to forecasted air quality. These are also posted at the Heart Butte Post Office.

Weather: A FIRE WEATHER WATCH is in effect for Saturday afternoon through Saturday evening for low humidity and gusty winds. Another busy fire weather period is expected over the next few days. Today will be very warm/hot with low to very low humidity. It will be breezy this afternoon. A few dry thunderstorms may develop this afternoon as well. A better chance of showers and thunderstorms is expected on Saturday with a mix of wet and dry thunderstorms, some with gusty outflow winds in excess of 45 mph. This will be followed by increasing winds late Saturday into Sunday.

Public Meeting: Wednesday, September 13, 2017 at 6 p.m. at the Heart Butte School-Multi-Purpose Room

Additional Information: Due to recent forest area closures around Strawberry Creek, Scalp, and Crucifixion Creek fires, the Pondera County Commissioners closed Swift Dam Campground west of Dupuyer yesterday evening. Adjacent fires affecting Rocky Mountain Ranger District include the Strawberry and Scalp Fires. For more detailed information, visit Inciweb or contact the Spotted Bear Ranger District: 406-758-5376.

  • Strawberry Fire: about 1,340 acres. Scalp Fire: about 11,425 acres. Due to poor visibility from smoke inversions set up throughout yesterday around the fires, ground personnel were unable to view fire behavior. An area closure went into effect for public safety on the Flathead and Helena-Lewis and Clark National Forests. Structure protection at Sabido Cabin was completed yesterday evening. 4 Personnel are conducting fuel reduction operations around the Wrong Creek Cabin today.
