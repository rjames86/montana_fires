September 4, 2017

FACT SHEET

DATE OF DETECTION: Saturday, July 22, 2017

CAUSE: Lightning

CURRENT ·ESTIMATED· SIZE: 17,480 acres

CONTAINMENT: 0%

  • Due to the fire’s rapid growth the past two days (10,040 acres) the fire’s containment has dropped from 5% down 0%. This metric is a measure of how close firefighters are to achieving management objectives, which include protecting and minimizing damage to private property and forest system lands.

  • An infrared flight took place at 11:30 p.m. last night. Shortly after, a cold front moved across the area, blowing the fire larger than what the infrared imagery showed on the map boundaries today. A new image of the fire will be taken tonight. The fire’s size is expected to increase tomorrow as a result of more accurate mapping.

  • Crews are constructing containment line in the Alice Creek, Tom’s Gulch and Wildcat drainages. A feller buncher, supported by hand crews are removing hazardous trees and woody debris created by the machine. This work will take several days to complete. Along the eastern flank, dozers and crews are working near Green and Bedrock Creeks.

  • Fire managers are working with landowners in the northeastern flank to locate areas to support air operations and to construct dozer and hand lines to protect private property.

  • Clean air early this morning allowed heavy helicopters to launch early. The provided water drops throughout the day. An air tanker also dropped retardant in Alice Creek to slow the fire’s movement south. This retardant will be re-enforced with ground firefighters in the days ahead.

  • Another day of cooler temperatures, higher humidity and light winds will assist firefighters with containment efforts. What growth does occur will be a result of light, terrain driven winds.

  • Evacuation orders remain in effect for the Landers Fork, Elk Trail, Elk Meadows/Evergreen Subdivisions, as well as the Alice Creek Basin and Tom’s Gulch areas. There are approximately 90 residents affected by the evacuation orders. The Dearborn Canyon residents have NOT received a pre-evacuation notice; however, they should be ready to leave, should conditions rapidly change.

  • There have been four confirmed structures located at the end of Bedrock Road that have been lost. No other structures have been reported. The Lewis and Clark County Sheriff is an essential partner in helping firefighters with this work and will be the notifying owners if structures have been destroyed.

  • Lewis and Clark County Sheriff is authorizing limited and controlled access to the properties for Elk Meadow residents who need access to their property to recover essential items. Residents will be escorted in and out by the Sheriff. Please call 406-447-8293.

Since August 30, the fire has grown approximately 11,339 acres. This is approximately 18 square miles.

  • The Forest expanded the area closure as a result of the aggressive and widespread fire activity occurring across the Forest. A map of the Closure Order can be found at: https://inciweb.nwcg.gov/incident/article/5410/39087/

  • A Red Cross Shelter is on standby at the Wolf Creek Elementary School (150 Walsh St, Wolf Creek, MT). Area residents that may need assistance are encouraged to contact Red Cross at 1-800-272-6668.

  • To sign up to receive emergency notification messages from the Lewis and Clark County Sheriff, register for Smart911 at: https://www.smart911.com/smart911/registration/registrationLanding.action
